Summary:
This bill expands and revises the criteria that local law enforcement agencies must satisfy before requesting an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth. Agencies must now determine they have used all available local resources and that there is information which, if disseminated, could aid in safe recovery. Once the California Highway Patrol (CHP) concurs, it must activate the alert. CHP must also submit a report to the Governor and Legislature by July 1, 2027. Local agencies—city police departments, county sheriffs’ offices, special district law enforcement units, and joint powers authorities—must update policies, train personnel, document new determinations, and coordinate with CHP under the new mandatory activation requirement.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: City police departments must revise missing‐persons protocols to include mandatory new determinations (use of all available local resources; existence of recoverable information) before requesting an Ebony Alert, document those determinations, and coordinate with CHP for mandatory alert activation. County: County sheriffs’ offices must update their missing‐persons policies to incorporate the expanded statutory criteria, document compliance with those criteria, and work with CHP to secure mandatory activation of an Ebony Alert upon concurrence. Special District: Special district law enforcement units (e.g., transit, water, or port police) must align their missing‐persons response protocols with the new Ebony Alert criteria, document compliance, and liaise with CHP for mandatory alert issuance upon concurrence. Joint Powers Authority: Regional law enforcement JPAs must adopt the revised Ebony Alert eligibility criteria into their interagency missing‐persons protocols, document the required determinations, and coordinate with CHP to secure mandatory alert activation.

Key Action Items

  • Revise all missing‐persons policies and procedures to incorporate the new Ebony Alert criteria
  • Develop documentation templates to record determinations regarding use of local resources and recoverable information
  • Train all relevant law enforcement personnel on the revised requirements and mandatory activation process
  • Designate a liaison or point of contact for CHP coordination on Ebony Alert requests and data sharing
  • Establish a tracking system for Ebony Alert activations and results to support the 2027 CHP report

Deadlines

  • 2027-07-01: California Highway Patrol must submit a report to the Governor’s Office and the Legislature on the activation, usage, and effectiveness of the Ebony Alert system. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Law enforcement agencies must determine they have used all available local resources before requesting an Ebony Alert
  • Agencies must verify that information exists which, if publicized, could aid in the safe recovery of the missing person
  • CHP must activate the Ebony Alert upon concurring with the requesting agency’s determinations
  • Local agencies must document and retain records of all required determinations and communications with CHP
  • Local agencies should supply data and cooperation for the CHP report due July 1, 2027

Summary:
This bill expands the High-Speed Rail Authority Inspector General’s (IG) audit and investigatory authority to cover any public or private entity receiving high-speed rail project funds, defines “personnel issues,” extends whistleblower protections to contractor employees, and requires inclusion of specified IG access provisions in all contracts and amendments executed on or after July 1, 2024. Local agencies that contract with or receive funds from the Authority—cities, counties, special districts, and joint powers authorities—must update contract templates, extend whistleblower protections, enable IG record and property access, and prepare for potential audits or investigations. Compliance is mandatory for all qualifying contracts as of July 1, 2024.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities that enter into contracts or interagency agreements with the High-Speed Rail Authority involving project funds must include IG access provisions in all new contracts or amendments executed on or after July 1, 2024. Cities and their contractors become subject to IG audits and investigations regarding adherence to contract provisions and must extend whistleblower protections to contractor employees. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) County: Counties receiving high-speed rail project funds—either as direct contractors or via intergovernmental agreements—must ensure all contracts and amendments executed on or after July 1, 2024 include the IG’s access provisions and must comply with IG audit and investigatory requests. County-employed contractors’ staff must receive extended whistleblower protections. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Special District: Independent special districts that contract with the Authority for design, construction, or operation services funded by the project must include provisions granting the IG access to records, property, and personnel as of July 1, 2024. They and their contractors are subject to expanded IG reviews and must extend whistleblower protections to contractor staff. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Joint Powers Authority: JPAs formed by local agencies to participate in HSR-funded projects must include the IG’s access and inspection provisions in all contracts or amendments using high-speed rail funds executed on or after July 1, 2024. JPAs and their contractors will be subject to IG investigations and must provide whistleblower protections to contractor employees. (Deadline: July 01, 2024)

Key Action Items

  • Inventory all existing and pending HSR-funded contracts or amendments; identify which require IG access provisions.
  • Revise standard contract templates and procurement checklists to include the IG’s inspection and access clauses effective July 1, 2024.
  • Update agency whistleblower policies to extend protections to employees of any contractor receiving HSR project funds.
  • Train contract managers and legal staff on new IG access rights, audit protocols, and response timelines.
  • Establish a central records-management protocol for rapid assembly and delivery of documents to the Inspector General upon request.

Deadlines

  • 2024-07-01: All contracts and contract amendments involving HSR project funds must include Inspector General access and inspection provisions. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Include IG access, audit, and investigatory clauses in all new HSR-related contracts and amendments executed on or after July 1, 2024.
  • Extend High-Speed Rail Authority whistleblower protections to employees of all contractors receiving project funds.

This bill amends the Reconnecting Communities: Highways to Boulevards Pilot Program administered by Caltrans, changing the funding split from a fixed 25% for planning and 75% for implementation to a flexible ‘up to 25%’ for planning with the balance for implementation. Local governments that apply for and administer these grants—cities, counties, special districts, and joint powers authorities—will have greater flexibility in allocating grant dollars toward implementation. Affected agencies must update grant applications, internal budgets, and compliance processes to reflect the new allocation rules.

  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:

Cities must revise grant proposals, project scopes, and internal budget documents to align with the new formula. County: Counties partnering with Caltrans on highway-to-boulevard conversions can now designate less than 25% of grant awards to planning and use a greater share for implementation. County public works and planning departments must adjust grant strategies and budget forecasts accordingly. Special District: Transportation and public works special districts eligible for the pilot program gain discretion to allocate up to 25% to planning, with the remainder devoted to implementation. District boards and finance staff must update internal grant management policies to reflect this change. Joint Powers Authority: Joint powers authorities formed for regional transportation projects may apply for pilot program grants with up to 25% allocated to planning. JPAs must revise interagency agreements and financial plans to capture the increased implementation funding opportunity.

  • Coordinate with Caltrans to revise project budgets and scopes to maximize implementation funding under the pilot program.
  • Train relevant staff (public works, planning, finance) on the revised allocation flexibility and compliance requirements.
  • Monitor legislative appropriations and Caltrans notices to track application deadlines and funding availability for upcoming grant cycles.

Additional Requirements

  • Adjust grant proposals to incorporate the updated allocation formula: planning (up to 25%) and implementation (remainder).

  • Maintain documentation demonstrating compliance with the revised program set-aside requirements.
