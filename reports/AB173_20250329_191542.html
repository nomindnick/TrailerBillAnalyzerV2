Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 2.

Summary:
This legislative change directs state transportation leadership to review and reallocate state funds for grade separation projects that have lost prior funding due to the Budget Act of 2024. The Secretary of Transportation is required to coordinate with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission and report on the funding situation by April 30, 2025. There is no direct imposition or compliance requirement on local public agencies.

Local Agencies Impacted

No local agencies identified.

Impact Description:
No direct impact on local agencies.

Key Action Items

  • Monitor updates from state transportation agencies regarding the allocation of funds for grade separation projects.
  • Review the report due by April 30, 2025, to assess any potential indirect impact on local funding commitments.

Deadlines

  • 2025-04-30: Deadline for the Secretary of Transportation to report to the Legislature on the funding identified for grade separation projects.

Additional Requirements

  • The Secretary must coordinate with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission to identify available funding.
  • Prioritize and allocate identified funds for grade separation projects at risk of losing federal and local funding or experiencing schedule delays.

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 13.

Summary:
The legislative change allows lead applicant agencies under the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to apply for a letter of no prejudice. This letter permits an agency to expend its own funds on port-specific projects with the prospect of later reimbursement if eligibility conditions are met. The mechanism is available only until it becomes inoperative on 2033-06-30 and is fully repealed on 2034-01-01, so affected agencies must adjust their funding strategies accordingly.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities that act as lead applicant agencies for port-related projects can apply for a letter of no prejudice to use their own funds subject to future reimbursement, provided they meet eligibility conditions. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) County: Counties administering or operating eligible port projects can access the new funding mechanism to use self-funding for projects, with reimbursement possible if conditions are met. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) Special District: Special Districts involved in managing port-specific projects may utilize the letter of no prejudice to expend their own funds, provided they comply with the program's reimbursement conditions. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities involved in port projects can leverage this provision to access self-funding options through the letter of no prejudice, subject to later reimbursement upon satisfying program conditions. (Deadline: June 30, 2033)

Key Action Items

  • Assess agency eligibility to act as a lead applicant under the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program.
  • Prepare and submit an application for a letter of no prejudice before June 30, 2033.
  • Establish robust financial tracking and documentation systems for eligible project expenditures.
  • Review and update funding strategies in anticipation of the repeal of the mechanism on January 1, 2034.

Deadlines

  • 2033-06-30: The date by which the letter of no prejudice mechanism becomes inoperative; applications must be submitted by this date to benefit from the provision. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)
  • 2034-01-01: The repeal date when the provisions authorizing the letter of no prejudice are fully removed from law. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Submit a letter of no prejudice application before the 2033-06-30 deadline.
  • Maintain detailed financial records to support potential future reimbursement.
  • Reassess and adjust funding strategies in advance of the repeal effective 2034-01-01.

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Public Finance (primary) - This change directly impacts the funding processes and reimbursement conditions for public agencies that use their own funds for infrastructure projects.
  • Municipal (secondary) - Agencies such as cities, counties, and special districts must adjust their strategies and practices in light of the new funding mechanism and upcoming repeal deadlines.

Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 4.

Summary:
The legislative change modifies state fleet requirements by mandating that the Department of Transportation compile and submit an annual report—with analysis of vehicle duty performance and details on charging/refueling infrastructure—on zero‐emission vehicles acquired by the department. The change continues existing state fleet purchasing targets but does not impose new or direct operational obligations on local public agencies such as cities, counties, or school districts.

Local Agencies Impacted

No local agencies identified.

Impact Description:
No direct impact on local agencies.

Key Action Items

  • Monitor state agency guidelines and reporting outcomes related to zero‐emission fleet requirements.
  • Review internal fleet management and procurement policies to prepare for potential indirect impacts from state-level changes.

Deadlines

Date Description Affected Agencies
2025-10-01 Annual deadline for the Department of Transportation to submit the zero-emission vehicle report (including performance analysis and charging/refueling station information) to the Legislature. All applicable agencies
2025-12-31 Effective start date for the requirement that 15% of newly purchased heavy-duty vehicles (GVWR 19,000+ lbs) for state fleets be zero-emission. All applicable agencies
2036-01-01 Repeal date for the new reporting provisions. All applicable agencies

Additional Requirements

  • Department of Transportation must compile and report detailed information on zero-emission vehicles (purchase numbers, weight class breakdown, performance analysis, and infrastructure details) to the Legislature annually by October 1, starting in 2025.
  • Maintain current state fleet purchase targets: at least 15% of heavy-duty vehicles and 50% of light-duty vehicles purchased for the state fleet must be zero-emission vehicles according to existing law.

Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 12.

Summary:
The legislative change adjusts the funding allocation in the Reconnecting Communities: Highways to Boulevards Pilot Program. Previously, 25% of available funds were reserved for planning and 75% for implementation; now, up to 25% may be allocated for planning with the remainder reserved for implementation. This change alters the program’s funding structure without imposing new statutory or compliance requirements on local public agencies.

No direct impact on local agencies.

Additional Requirements

  • Review and comply with updated grant application guidelines from the Department of Transportation.

Relevant Practice Groups
