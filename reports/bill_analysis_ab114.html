Summary:
This bill revises the alternative education grant formula for county offices of education (COEs) by increasing the base grant component, modifying the calculation of average daily attendance, and establishing add-ons of $200,000 for COEs operating juvenile court schools and another $200,000 for those operating county community schools. The bill affects only county offices of education, not other local public agencies. COEs will experience a positive financial impact through increased funding, which will require adjustments to their budget planning and reporting processes beginning with the 2023-24 fiscal year.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • County Offices of Education

Impact Description:
County Offices of Education: Increased funding through revised alternative education grant formula, including higher base grant amount, new $200,000 add-ons for juvenile court schools and county community schools, and modified average daily attendance calculations that allow using the higher of current year, prior year, or average of three prior years. (Deadline: July 01, 2023)

Key Action Items

  • Update budget planning documents for 2023-24 fiscal year
  • Adjust attendance tracking systems to accommodate new calculation methods
  • Revise local control and accountability plans to address new funding streams
  • Ensure proper accounting for the $200,000 add-ons for juvenile court schools and county community schools

Deadlines

  • 2023-07-01: Implementation of new alternative education grant formula and funding add-ons (Affected agencies: County Offices of Education)

Additional Requirements

  • Calculate alternative education grants using revised formula starting in 2023-24 fiscal year
  • Track average daily attendance using new methodology
  • Include juvenile court school and county community school add-ons in budget planning
  • Include new funding streams in local control and accountability plans

  • Governance (secondary) - COEs will need to make governance decisions about how to allocate and report on new funding streams

Instead of electing to participate through their governing board and notifying the State Controller, participating parties can now request a 'local intercept' through county treasurers or fiscal officers.
