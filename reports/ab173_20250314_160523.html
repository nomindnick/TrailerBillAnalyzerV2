Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SECTION 1.

Summary:
This bill modifies the Ebony Alert system by expanding and revising the criteria law enforcement agencies must meet before requesting an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth. It affects city police departments and county sheriff's departments by establishing more specific requirements for determining when to request an Ebony Alert, including verifying that all local resources have been utilized and that public dissemination of information could aid in recovery. The bill also makes CHP's activation of an Ebony Alert mandatory rather than discretionary when they concur with the law enforcement agency's determination.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • Counties (Sheriff's Departments)
  • Cities (Police Departments)

Impact Description:
Cities (Police Departments): Must follow revised criteria when requesting Ebony Alerts for missing Black youth, including verifying all local resources have been used and that public information dissemination would assist in recovery. Counties (Sheriff's Departments): Must follow revised criteria when requesting Ebony Alerts for missing Black youth, including verifying all local resources have been used and that public information dissemination would assist in recovery.

Key Action Items

  • Review and update internal policies and procedures related to requesting Ebony Alerts
  • Train law enforcement personnel on the new criteria for requesting Ebony Alerts
  • Develop documentation processes to demonstrate compliance with the new requirements

Additional Requirements

  • Law enforcement agencies must determine the missing person is between 12-25 years of age
  • Agencies must verify all available local resources have been used before requesting an alert
  • Agencies must determine that public information dissemination could assist in recovery
  • Agencies must determine either that the person went missing under unexplained/suspicious circumstances or that the person is in danger due to specific factors

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - Provides counsel to cities and counties whose law enforcement agencies must implement the revised Ebony Alert request procedures
  • Governance (secondary) - May need to advise on policy updates related to missing persons alerts and protocols

Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 12.

Summary:
This bill modifies the Reconnecting Communities: Highways to Boulevards Pilot Program by changing the funding allocation from a fixed 25% for planning and 75% for implementation to 'up to 25%' for planning with the remainder for implementation. This change affects cities, counties, special districts (particularly transportation-related), joint powers authorities (JPAs), and transit agencies that are eligible to apply for these competitive grants. These local public agencies now have more flexibility in how funds might be allocated between planning and implementation phases, potentially allowing more funding to flow to implementation projects if the full 25% isn't needed for planning.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • Cities and Counties
  • Joint Powers Authorities
  • Transportation-related Special Districts

Impact Description:
Cities and Counties: As eligible applicants for both planning and implementation grants, cities and counties will benefit from the increased flexibility in funding allocation between planning and implementation projects. Transportation-related Special Districts: Special districts involved in transportation can apply for grants with the understanding that more implementation funding may be available than under the previous fixed allocation. Joint Powers Authorities: JPAs are eligible for both planning and implementation grants and will operate under more flexible funding allocations when applying. Transit Agencies: Transit agencies are specifically eligible for planning grants and will benefit from the potential for more implementation funding if planning needs are less than 25% of total program funds.

Key Action Items

  • Review current and planned grant applications to understand the new funding flexibility
  • Consider adjusting planning/implementation funding requests in applications based on the new allocation structure
  • Stay informed about any guideline updates from the Department of Transportation resulting from this change

Additional Requirements

  • 100% of program funds must benefit underserved communities
  • Projects must be developed in partnership with the Department of Transportation
  • Projects must demonstrate consistency with federal Reconnecting Communities Pilot Program requirements
  • Applications for implementation grants must show the ability to successfully compete for federal grant funding

Summary:
This bill creates a 'letter of no prejudice' process for the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program, allowing lead applicant agencies to spend their own funds on approved projects with the possibility of future state reimbursement if certain conditions are met. This impacts local public agencies that administer or operate port and freight infrastructure projects, including port authorities, cities, counties, special districts, and Joint Powers Authorities (JPAs). These agencies gain increased flexibility to advance approved port and transportation projects using local funds when state funding might be delayed, with a pathway to reimbursement once state funds become available.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • Port authorities (special districts)
  • Joint Powers Authorities (JPAs)
  • Cities and counties with port facilities

Impact Description:
Port authorities (special districts): Can advance approved port infrastructure projects using their own funds, with potential reimbursement from state program funds later (Deadline: June 30, 2033) Cities and counties with port facilities: Can expedite port-related projects by spending local funds first, then seeking reimbursement when state funds are available (Deadline: June 30, 2033) Joint Powers Authorities (JPAs): JPAs managing port or transportation projects can utilize the letter of no prejudice process to advance projects with local funding (Deadline: June 30, 2033)

Key Action Items

  • Review eligibility for the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program
  • Develop internal procedures for applying for letters of no prejudice
  • Create financial planning for potential self-funding of approved projects
  • Prepare for negotiating reimbursement agreements with the Transportation Agency

Deadlines

  • 2033-06-30: Provisions authorizing letters of no prejudice become inoperative (Affected agencies: Port authorities, Cities, Counties, Joint Powers Authorities, Special districts involved in transportation)

Additional Requirements

  • Project must have commenced and regional/local expenditures incurred
  • Expenditures must be eligible for reimbursement under applicable laws
  • Lead applicant agency must comply with all legal requirements, including CEQA
  • Sufficient program funds must be available for reimbursement
  • Lead applicant agency must enter into a reimbursement agreement with the Transportation Agency
