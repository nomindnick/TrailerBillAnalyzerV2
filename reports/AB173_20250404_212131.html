Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 2.

Summary:
This legislation requires the Secretary of Transportation to identify alternative funding for grade separation projects that lost previous funding due to budget reversions in 2024. The Transportation Agency must prioritize projects at risk of losing federal/local funding or facing schedule delays, and report to the Legislature by April 30, 2025. This change impacts local agencies that sponsor or benefit from grade separation projects, particularly cities, counties, and special districts involved in transportation infrastructure.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities that had grade separation projects with funding reverted under the Budget Act of 2024 may be eligible for alternative state funding. This presents an opportunity for cities to secure replacement funding for at-risk projects, particularly those with federal matching requirements or facing schedule delays. (Deadline: April 30, 2025) County: Counties with grade separation projects that lost funding due to the 2024 budget reversions may qualify for replacement funding. The Transportation Agency is authorized to directly allocate funds to prioritized projects, creating a pathway for counties to secure support for critical infrastructure. (Deadline: April 30, 2025) Special District: Transportation-focused special districts (e.g., transit districts, transportation authorities) that sponsor grade separation projects affected by 2024 funding reversions may be eligible for alternative state funding. This provides an opportunity to salvage projects at risk of losing matching funds or facing delays. (Deadline: April 30, 2025) Joint Powers Authority: JPAs formed for transportation infrastructure purposes may benefit from this legislation if they oversee grade separation projects that lost funding. The direct allocation authority granted to the Transportation Agency creates a mechanism for JPAs to secure alternative funding for critical projects. (Deadline: April 30, 2025)

Key Action Items

  • Review transportation project portfolios to identify grade separation projects affected by 2024 funding reversions
  • Document and quantify risks to federal or local funding commitments for affected projects
  • Prepare evidence of potential schedule delays that could result from funding gaps
  • Engage with Transportation Agency, Caltrans, and California Transportation Commission regarding funding needs
  • Monitor the Transportation Agency's process for identifying and allocating alternative funding
  • Prepare funding requests that align with the prioritization criteria established by the Transportation Agency

Deadlines

  • 2025-04-30: Deadline for the Secretary of Transportation to report to the Legislature on funding identified for impacted grade separation projects (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Identify grade separation projects that had funding reverted pursuant to the Budget Act of 2024
  • Document risks to federal and local funding commitments
  • Provide evidence of potential project schedule delays
  • Engage with state transportation officials to highlight project needs and funding gaps
  • Monitor Transportation Agency announcements regarding alternative funding opportunities

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - This practice group directly supports cities, counties, and special districts that may need to navigate the process of securing alternative funding for their grade separation projects.
  • Public Finance (primary) - The legislation creates potential funding opportunities that will require expertise in public agency financing, particularly for complex infrastructure projects with multiple funding sources.
  • Facilities and Business (secondary) - Grade separation projects are major facilities investments, and this practice group would support local agencies in addressing the business and facility aspects of these infrastructure projects.
  • Litigation (secondary) - Potential disputes could arise related to funding allocations, project prioritization, or contracts affected by funding changes, requiring litigation support.

It creates new requirements for RTAs to submit updated regional short-term financial plans and transit operator data to the Transportation Agency to receive funding. These requirements indirectly impact local transit agencies operated by cities, counties, and special districts that receive formula-based transit funding.

(Deadline: July 01, 2025) City: Cities that operate transit services will be indirectly affected as they will need to provide transit operator data to their regional transportation planning agency (RTPA) for inclusion in the region's submission to the Transportation Agency. It extends whistleblower protections to contractor employees and requires future contracts (from July 1, 2024) to include provisions giving the Inspector General access to records. While primarily focused on state-level oversight, this impacts local agencies that may contract with the High-Speed Rail Authority or receive project funds.

  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

New contracts executed after July 1, 2024 must include provisions granting the Inspector General access to records and property. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) County: Counties that enter into contracts with the High-Speed Rail Authority or receive high-speed rail project funds will be subject to additional oversight by the Inspector General, including audits and investigations of contract adherence. New contracts executed after July 1, 2024 must include provisions granting the Inspector General access to records and property. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Special District: Special districts (particularly transportation or infrastructure-related districts) that enter into contracts with the High-Speed Rail Authority or receive high-speed rail project funds will be subject to additional oversight by the Inspector General, including audits and investigations of contract adherence. New contracts executed after July 1, 2024 must include provisions granting the Inspector General access to records and property. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities (particularly those involved in transportation or infrastructure) that enter into contracts with the High-Speed Rail Authority or receive high-speed rail project funds will be subject to additional oversight by the Inspector General, including audits and investigations of contract adherence. New contracts executed after July 1, 2024 must include provisions granting the Inspector General access to records and property. (Deadline: July 01, 2024)

Special District: Special districts (particularly transportation-related districts) may be eligible entities for this program and affected by the change in funding allocation. Summary:
This legislation modifies the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to allow lead applicant agencies to request letters of no prejudice from the Secretary of Transportation. This mechanism enables agencies to use their own funds for approved port infrastructure projects with the possibility of future reimbursement from program funds. The provision expires on June 30, 2033, and is repealed January 1, 2034.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities that operate ports or administer port-related infrastructure projects can now apply for letters of no prejudice to advance project timelines using their own funds with potential for later reimbursement from state program moneys. County: Counties that administer port or freight infrastructure projects can utilize the letter of no prejudice mechanism to expedite project implementation using local funds while preserving eligibility for state reimbursement. Special District: Port districts, harbor districts, and transportation-related special districts can advance approved port and freight infrastructure projects with local funding while maintaining eligibility for later state reimbursement through the letter of no prejudice process. Joint Powers Authority: Transportation or port-related JPAs that administer eligible port and freight infrastructure projects can use the letter of no prejudice mechanism to advance projects using their own funding sources with potential for future state reimbursement.

Key Action Items

  • Review existing and planned port/freight infrastructure projects for potential acceleration using the letter of no prejudice mechanism
  • Develop internal procedures for evaluating financial capacity to advance project funds
  • Create template applications for letters of no prejudice to streamline the process
  • Establish accounting practices to track expenditures eligible for future reimbursement
  • Consider bond or other financing mechanisms to fund upfront costs of eligible projects

Deadlines

  • 2033-06-30: Last date to use the letter of no prejudice mechanism before provisions become inoperative (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Project must be included in the approved program of projects awarded by the Transportation Agency
  • Agency must have financial capacity to fund project components upfront
  • Expenditures must meet program eligibility criteria to qualify for future reimbursement
  • Application for letter of no prejudice must be submitted to and approved by the Secretary of Transportation
