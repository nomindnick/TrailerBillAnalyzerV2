Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 2.

Summary:
The legislative change directs the Secretary of Transportation to work with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission to identify state funds for grade separation projects that risk losing local or federal financial commitments or falling behind schedule. Although the changes are implemented at the state level, they indirectly impact local agencies involved in transportation infrastructure by altering funding dynamics, coordination requirements, and project planning expectations.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities that participate in or contribute to grade separation projects may see shifts in their local funding roles as the state agency reallocates funds to cover projects at risk. This may require cities to review their budget commitments, update local funding arrangements, and actively coordinate with state transportation officials. (Deadline: April 30, 2025) County: County governments that support transportation infrastructure projects are likely to need reassessment of their local funding contributions given the state’s new authority in directly allocating funds. This requires counties to reexamine project priorities and potentially update inter-agency funding agreements. (Deadline: April 30, 2025) Special District: Transportation-related Special Districts could be indirectly affected as their funding participation might change with the reallocation of state funds. These districts should re-evaluate their agreements and financial commitments to grade separation projects. Joint Powers Authority: JPAs formed by local agencies to jointly manage transportation projects may face changes to their funding mixes and collaboration frameworks. They should review joint funding arrangements as the state directly allocates funds for projects at risk.

Key Action Items

  • Local agencies should conduct a comprehensive review of their funding commitments for grade separation projects.
  • Establish or enhance communication channels with state transportation agencies to align project priorities and secure necessary funding.
  • Monitor the forthcoming legislative report (due by April 30, 2025) to assess further impacts on local funding roles and project deadlines.

Deadlines

  • 2025-04-30: Deadline for the Secretary of Transportation to report to the Legislature regarding available state funding for grade separation projects. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Review and update local funding obligations for transportation projects.
  • Coordinate with state agencies to understand new funding allocation methods.
  • Update inter-agency agreements and project planning frameworks in line with state funding priorities.

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - This group advises cities, counties, and similar local agencies on governance and funding issues, making it central for addressing changes in local infrastructure project coordination and budgeting.
  • Public Finance (secondary) - Public Finance is key in analyzing and advising on the implications of state funding reallocations on local financing strategies and intergovernmental funding commitments.
  • Facilities and Business (secondary) - Given the focus on capital projects and infrastructure improvements, this group can assist local agencies in managing changes to facility-related financing and operational requirements.

It requires regional transportation planning agencies to update their regional short‐term financial plans and transit operator data in order to access funding for the 2025–26 fiscal year and beyond. Although the changes are directed at state-level procedures, local agencies involved in transit operations—such as cities, counties, special districts, and joint powers authorities—face indirect impacts. Summary:
The legislative change advances California’s commitment to reducing greenhouse gases by mandating a shift to zero‐emission vehicles for government fleets. Although many requirements target state agencies (e.g., minimum percentages for heavy- and light-duty vehicles and an annual reporting requirement by the Department of Transportation), the Advanced Clean Fleets Regulation also applies to local government fleets. This means that cities, counties, special districts, and joint powers authorities that operate medium- and heavy-duty trucks or other high-priority fleets must prepare to update their procurement policies, fleet management practices, and potentially their infrastructure (e.g., EV charging/refueling stations) in order to align with state mandates and guidelines.

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities with fleets that include medium- and heavy-duty trucks may need to revise procurement processes and operational policies to align with state mandates on zero-emission vehicle acquisition. This may also involve planning for necessary infrastructure improvements and ensuring coordination with state reporting mechanisms. (Deadline: December 31, 2025) County: County agencies that manage fleets with medium- and heavy-duty trucks must begin planning for a transition to zero-emission vehicles. This includes reassessing vehicle replacement schedules, updating procurement guidelines, and aligning internal policies with emerging state-level mandates. (Deadline: December 31, 2025) Special District: Special districts that operate fleets—such as those for public transit, water, or sanitation—may be indirectly affected by the state's zero-emission mandates. They will need to assess their fleet composition, update procurement processes, and plan for upgrades to supporting infrastructure. (Deadline: December 31, 2025) Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities coordinating services across multiple local entities may also have fleets that fall under the new mandates. They will need to harmonize policies among member agencies, update fleet management practices, and possibly institute reporting mechanisms consistent with state requirements. (Deadline: December 31, 2025)

Key Action Items

  • Conduct a comprehensive inventory of fleet vehicles to determine which fall under the new zero-emission requirements.
  • Review and update procurement policies to incorporate state zero-emission mandates.
  • Develop and implement a detailed transition plan for moving towards a zero-emission fleet, including timelines and budget estimates.
  • Coordinate with state agencies (e.g., Department of Transportation) to obtain guidance on compliance, reporting standards, and potential funding support.
  • Plan and budget for infrastructure improvements such as installation of electric vehicle charging or refueling stations.

Deadlines

  • 2025-10-01: Deadline for the Department of Transportation to submit its annual report on zero-emission vehicles, which may indirectly influence local agencies’ reporting and compliance practices. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)
  • 2025-12-31: Effective date for the state-level mandate requiring that at least 15% of new heavy-duty vehicle purchases be zero-emission. Local agencies should consider aligning their procurement policies in anticipation of similar requirements. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Update fleet procurement policies to align with new zero-emission targets.
  • Develop and implement a fleet transition plan including compliance tracking and reporting.
  • Invest in and plan for necessary infrastructure upgrades, such as EV charging and refueling stations.
  • Coordinate with state agencies for technical guidance and potential funding opportunities.

  • Public Finance (secondary) - The transition to zero-emission fleets may involve significant capital expenditure and budgetary adjustments, making funding strategies and financial planning critical for affected local agencies.

(Deadline: July 01, 2024) Special District: Special Districts contracting for high-speed rail project funds will be required to incorporate specific IG access provisions in their agreements, subjecting them to the expanded scrutiny and compliance checks. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities formed by local agencies for high-speed rail projects will need to update contracts to include provisions that grant the Inspector General the access necessary for conducting audits and reviews. Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 13.

Summary:
The legislative change modifies the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program by authorizing a lead applicant agency to apply for a letter of no prejudice. This letter, once approved by the Secretary of Transportation, will allow an agency to expend its own funds on port-specific projects (or components thereof) with the prospect of future reimbursement for eligible expenditures if certain conditions are met. The change impacts local public agencies that act as lead applicants in port/freight projects by altering funding and reimbursement mechanisms. Note that these provisions are temporary – they become inoperative on 2033-06-30 and are repealed on 2034-01-01.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities serving as lead applicant agencies in port-related projects can now apply for a letter of no prejudice, allowing them to use their own funds upfront with a view to future reimbursement. They must comply with specific eligibility and documentation requirements to secure the benefit. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) County: Counties that administer or operate eligible port-specific projects can now leverage the new funding option by applying for a letter of no prejudice. This change allows for self-financing with later reimbursement, subject to stringent reporting and compliance standards. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) Special District: Special Districts, including port authorities, may serve as lead applicant agencies under the program. They now have the option to utilize their own funds for approved projects if they obtain a letter of no prejudice, which mandates adherence to reimbursement conditions and proper financial oversight. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities formed by collaborative local agencies can act as lead applicants for port-related infrastructure projects. They are now empowered to use their own funds upfront by applying for a letter of no prejudice, ensuring coordinated self-financing with eventual reimbursement if conditions are met. (Deadline: June 30, 2033)

Key Action Items

  • Review current port and freight project plans to determine eligibility as a lead applicant under the new provision.
  • Develop and implement internal processes for applying for the letter of no prejudice with the necessary documentation.
  • Adjust project funding timelines to ensure all expenditures using personal funds occur before the inoperative date of 2033-06-30.
  • Ensure compliance with all program guidelines to secure future reimbursement for eligible expenses.

Deadlines

  • 2033-06-30: The new funding provisions become inoperative on this date. Local agencies must apply for and execute projects under the letter of no prejudice option before this deadline. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)
  • 2034-01-01: The provisions are repealed effective this date, meaning the option for self-financed projects with future reimbursement will no longer be available. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Apply for the letter of no prejudice through the Secretary of Transportation or designee.
  • Maintain thorough documentation and records of eligible project expenditures.
  • Adhere to the established guidelines and conditions for reimbursement.
  • Plan and execute project funding strategies within the designated timeframe.

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - Cities, counties, and special districts that are directly involved in administering port-related projects will require guidance on the new funding mechanism and administrative compliance issues.
  • Public Finance (primary) - The change involves a new financial arrangement—enabling advance expenditure and later reimbursement—which falls squarely within public financing and funding compliance matters.
