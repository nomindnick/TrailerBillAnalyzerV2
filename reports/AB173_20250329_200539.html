Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 2.

Summary:
This legislative change mandates that the Secretary of Transportation, in coordination with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission, identify and prioritize state funding for grade separation projects that risk losing federal and local funding or are facing schedule delays. While the law targets state-level agencies, it indirectly impacts local agencies involved in such projects by altering funding dynamics, oversight, and compliance requirements.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities that manage or participate in grade separation projects may face altered funding roles as state funds are reallocated to cover shortfalls. This could lead to adjustments in local budgeting, project planning, and coordination with state agencies. County: Counties involved in infrastructure projects, including grade separations, need to accommodate changes in financial responsibilities. They may have to adjust local funding commitments and participate in state-led project reviews to ensure continued project viability. Special District: Special districts that manage transportation or related services may be indirectly affected as state funds become available to support grade separation projects, potentially relieving some local funding pressures but also requiring alignment with state criteria. Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities formed by multiple local agencies will need to adapt to new state funding allocation processes. This may involve coordinating shared reporting requirements and revising project governance frameworks to comply with state oversight.

Key Action Items

  • Conduct a review of current grade separation projects to assess funding vulnerabilities and identify potential state funding opportunities.
  • Establish or enhance coordination mechanisms with state transportation agencies to ensure timely and accurate reporting of project data.
  • Adjust local budgeting and project planning processes to integrate potential changes in funding responsibilities resulting from state reallocation.

Deadlines

  • 2025-04-30: Deadline for the Secretary of Transportation to report to the Legislature on the funding identified for grade separation projects that are at risk due to potential loss of federal and local funding commitments or schedule delays. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Local agencies must establish effective communication and data-sharing protocols with state agencies to verify project eligibility and funding needs.
  • Review and update local project oversight and funding strategies in anticipation of state-level reallocation of funds for grade separation projects.

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - Local government entities such as cities, counties, special districts, and joint powers authorities are directly involved in infrastructure projects and will need to adjust operations in response to changes in funding allocations.
  • Public Finance (primary) - The reallocation of state funds for grade separation projects directly impacts budgeting and financing practices, making expertise in public finance critical for these local agencies.
  • Facilities and Business (secondary) - As funding reallocation may affect contractor work, facility management, and business operations related to construction and project management, a thorough understanding of facilities and business practices will be useful.

It imposes additional reporting and compliance requirements on regional transportation planning agencies—including updated regional short‐term financial plans and transit operator data submissions—in order to receive state funds in fiscal years 2025–26, 2026–27, and 2027–28. While the reporting requirement targets state agencies, the Advanced Clean Fleets Regulation also mandates the transition of local, state, and federal government fleets (including medium‐ and heavy‐duty vehicles) to zero‐emission models. Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 13.

Summary:
The legislative change allows lead applicant agencies under the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to request a letter of no prejudice from the Secretary of Transportation, enabling them to front project costs with their own funds and seek reimbursement later if eligible expenditures are met. This creates new financial flexibility but also imposes additional administrative, compliance, and record-keeping requirements for agencies involved in port-specific projects. The mechanism is temporary, becoming inoperative on 2033-06-30 and repealed on 2034-01-01.

  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:

This requires updating financial procedures and application processes. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) County: Counties involved in port and freight infrastructure projects are affected as they must now apply for the new mechanism to use their funds initially, with the expectation of reimbursement once conditions are met. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) Special District: Special Districts managing port-specific projects may utilize the new funding approach, allowing them to advance their own capital subject to later repayment. These agencies need to align internal procedures with the program's compliance requirements. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities engaged in collaborative port projects can take advantage of the advanced funding method. They must ensure effective coordination among member agencies and meet the new reimbursement conditions. (Deadline: June 30, 2033)

Key Action Items

  • Assess current financial procedures and update them to incorporate the letter of no prejudice process and eligibility for reimbursement.
  • Establish or revise tracking systems for documenting eligible project expenditures and ensure compliance with reimbursement requirements.
  • Coordinate internally and with state officials to understand application details and monitor the approaching inoperative and repeal deadlines.

Deadlines

  • 2033-06-30: The provisions allowing agencies to expend their own funds with the prospect of reimbursement will become inoperative. Agencies must complete any necessary applications or financial planning before this date. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)
  • 2034-01-01: The provisions are repealed, terminating the advanced funding mechanism entirely. Any planned strategy relying on this provision must be finalized before this date. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Implement and maintain comprehensive financial tracking and record-keeping protocols to document eligible project costs

  • Public Finance (primary) - The change directly affects financing and reimbursement processes, requiring detailed budgeting and financial planning adjustments by affected agencies.
  • Facilities and Business (secondary) - Agencies will need to manage contracts and procurement related to port infrastructure projects under the new funding scheme, impacting facility and business operations.

Summary:
The legislative change expands the oversight role of the High-Speed Rail Authority’s Inspector General by requiring reviews of contract compliance for any public or private entity that receives high-speed rail project funds. It imposes a new contractual requirement—effective for all contracts or amendments executed on or after July 1, 2024—to include provisions granting IG access to records and property. Additionally, it extends whistleblower protections to contractor employees. Although the changes primarily target state-level rail project oversight, local agencies that enter into contracts or receive funds for related projects may be indirectly impacted by new compliance obligations and audit scrutiny.

(Deadline: July 01, 2024) Special District: Special districts that participate in projects using high-speed rail funds are required to modify their contractual processes to include the new inspector general provisions, facing potential audit and review of their contract performance. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities that are party to contracts funded by high-speed rail resources must ensure that new IG access provisions are integrated into their agreements and that their compliance procedures are adjusted accordingly.
