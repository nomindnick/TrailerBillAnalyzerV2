Trailer Bill Sections: SECTION 1.

Summary:
This bill amends the Ebony Alert system for missing Black youth by expanding the criteria law enforcement agencies must meet to request an alert, making CHP activation mandatory when criteria are met, and requiring CHP to submit a report by July 1, 2027. This directly impacts local law enforcement agencies operated by cities and counties.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County

Impact Description:
City: City police departments must follow revised criteria when requesting Ebony Alerts for missing Black youth, including demonstrating they've exhausted local resources and have information that public dissemination could help recover the missing person. The CHP will now be required (not just authorized) to activate alerts when criteria are met. County: County sheriff departments must follow revised criteria when requesting Ebony Alerts for missing Black youth, including demonstrating they've exhausted local resources and have information that public dissemination could help recover the missing person. The CHP will now be required (not just authorized) to activate alerts when criteria are met.

Key Action Items

  • Review and update law enforcement policies regarding missing persons and Ebony Alert requests
  • Develop and conduct training for law enforcement personnel on the revised Ebony Alert criteria
  • Establish or enhance record-keeping systems for tracking Ebony Alert cases and outcomes
  • Create internal procedures to document that all local resources were exhausted before requesting an alert

Deadlines

  • 2027-07-01: Deadline for CHP to submit report to Governor and Legislature (local agencies should maintain proper records to support this reporting) (Affected agencies: City, County)

Additional Requirements

  • Demonstrate all available local resources have been used before requesting an Ebony Alert
  • Document information available that, if disseminated to the public, could assist in safe recovery
  • Comply with any CHP data collection requirements to support their mandatory reporting obligation

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - This practice group directly serves cities and counties whose law enforcement agencies must implement the revised Ebony Alert request criteria.
  • Governance (secondary) - The changes affect procedural governance of law enforcement agencies regarding when and how to request Ebony Alerts.
  • Litigation (secondary) - Potential litigation risks if law enforcement agencies fail to properly implement the new requirements for requesting Ebony Alerts.
