Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 2.

Summary:
This legislation requires the Secretary of Transportation to identify funding for grade separation projects that lost previously awarded funding due to the Budget Act of 2024. The Transportation Agency must prioritize projects at risk of losing federal/local funding or facing schedule delays, and can directly allocate available funds to these projects. The Secretary must report to the Legislature by April 30, 2025 about identified funding for impacted grade separation projects. This impacts local agencies that sponsor or implement grade separation projects.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities that had grade separation projects affected by funding reversions in the Budget Act of 2024 may be eligible for alternative state transportation funding. Cities that sponsor grade separation projects at risk of losing federal/local funding commitments or facing schedule delays should be prepared to work with the Transportation Agency to access potential funding. (Deadline: April 30, 2025) County: Counties that had grade separation projects affected by funding reversions in the Budget Act of 2024 may be eligible for alternative state transportation funding. Counties that sponsor grade separation projects at risk of losing federal/local funding commitments or facing schedule delays should be prepared to work with the Transportation Agency to access potential funding. (Deadline: April 30, 2025) Special District: Special districts (particularly transit or transportation districts) that had grade separation projects affected by funding reversions in the Budget Act of 2024 may be eligible for alternative state transportation funding. Special districts that sponsor grade separation projects at risk of losing federal/local funding commitments or facing schedule delays should be prepared to work with the Transportation Agency to access potential funding. (Deadline: April 30, 2025) Joint Powers Authority: JPAs involved in transportation projects that had grade separation projects affected by funding reversions in the Budget Act of 2024 may be eligible for alternative state transportation funding. JPAs that sponsor grade separation projects at risk of losing federal/local funding commitments or facing schedule delays should be prepared to work with the Transportation Agency to access potential funding. (Deadline: April 30, 2025)

Key Action Items

  • Review existing grade separation projects to identify those affected by funding reversions in the Budget Act of 2024
  • Assess risk factors for grade separation projects (potential loss of federal/local funding or schedule delays)
  • Prepare documentation demonstrating project eligibility and risk factors
  • Engage with Transportation Agency regarding potential funding opportunities
  • Monitor the Secretary's report to the Legislature (due April 30, 2025) for identified funding sources
  • Update capital improvement plans to account for potential new funding sources

Deadlines

  • 2025-04-30: Secretary of Transportation must report to the Legislature on funding identified for impacted grade separation projects (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Identify grade separation projects affected by Budget Act of 2024 funding reversions
  • Document risk factors (potential loss of federal/local funding or schedule delays)
  • Coordinate with Transportation Agency on alternative funding sources
  • Maintain current project documentation to facilitate potential funding requests
  • Monitor implementation of the legislation through the Secretary's report to the Legislature

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Facilities and Business (primary) - This practice group focuses on public agency business operations and facility-related issues, including infrastructure projects like grade separations, making it directly relevant to the funding mechanisms addressed in this legislation.

It requires regional transportation planning agencies to submit updated financial plans and transit operator data for fiscal years 2025-26 through 2027-28 to receive funding. This impacts special districts, counties, and cities that operate or oversee public transit systems or participate in regional transportation planning.

  • Joint Powers Authority

(Deadline: June 30, 2025) County: Counties that serve as regional transportation planning agencies or operate transit services must comply with new reporting requirements to access state transit funding. Counties must submit updated regional short-term financial plans for 2025-26 and transit operator data for fiscal years 2025-26 through 2027-28. (Deadline: June 30, 2025) City: Cities that operate municipal transit systems will need to provide operational data to their regional transportation planning agencies to maintain eligibility for state transit funding. (Deadline: June 30, 2025) Joint Powers Authority: JPAs that operate transit services or function as regional transportation planning agencies must comply with expanded accountability program requirements to maintain access to state transit funding, including submitting financial plans and operational data.   • Evaluate potential funding impacts from the expanded accountability program requirements

Summary:
This legislation expands the authority of the High-Speed Rail Authority Inspector General to review or investigate contract compliance by any entity receiving high-speed rail project funds. It extends whistleblower protections to contractor employees and requires contracts executed after July 1, 2024 to include provisions granting the Inspector General access to records. While primarily affecting state-level operations, this has indirect impacts on local agencies that may contract with or receive funds from the High-Speed Rail Authority.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities that contract with or receive funds from the High-Speed Rail Authority will be subject to expanded oversight by the Inspector General, including reviews of contract compliance and access to records requirements in new contracts after July 1, 2024. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) County: Counties that contract with or receive funds from the High-Speed Rail Authority will be subject to expanded oversight by the Inspector General, including reviews of contract compliance and access to records requirements in new contracts after July 1, 2024. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Special District: Special districts (such as transportation or utility districts) that contract with or receive funds from the High-Speed Rail Authority will be subject to expanded oversight by the Inspector General, including reviews of contract compliance and access to records requirements in new contracts after July 1, 2024. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Joint Powers Authority: JPAs that contract with or receive funds from the High-Speed Rail Authority (particularly transportation-related JPAs) will be subject to expanded oversight by the Inspector General, including reviews of contract compliance and access to records requirements in new contracts after July 1, 2024. (Deadline: July 01, 2024)

Key Action Items

  • Review any existing contracts with High-Speed Rail Authority to understand expanded oversight provisions
  • Update contract templates for agreements with High-Speed Rail Authority to include Inspector General access provisions for contracts after July 1, 2024
  • Inform staff about extended whistleblower protections for contractor employees
  • Develop procedures for responding to potential Inspector General audits or investigations
  • Assess record-keeping practices to ensure compliance with potential Inspector General reviews

Deadlines

  • 2024-07-01: New contracts or amendments with High-Speed Rail Authority must include provisions granting the Inspector General access to records and property (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

  • Prepare for compliance with Inspector General investigations or audits regarding adherence to contract provisions
  • Ensure whistleblower protections are extended to contractor employees working on High-Speed Rail projects
  • Maintain accessible records that may be subject to Inspector General review

This change affects local agencies that are eligible to apply for these competitive grants to convert underutilized state highways into multimodal corridors serving underserved communities.

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities are eligible entities that can apply for these competitive grants. The change provides more flexibility in funding allocation, potentially allowing more implementation funding if planning needs are less than 25% of the total. Cities partnering with Caltrans on highway conversion projects will be affected by this funding formula change. County: Counties are eligible entities that can apply for these competitive grants. The modified funding formula provides more discretion in allocating funds between planning and implementation phases, potentially allowing more implementation projects if planning needs are less than 25%. Special District: Transportation-related special districts may be eligible entities for these grants. The change in funding allocation affects how funds are distributed between planning and implementation phases of highway conversion projects. Joint Powers Authority: JPAs formed between local agencies for transportation purposes may be eligible to apply for these grants. The modification to the funding allocation creates more flexibility in how funds are distributed between planning and implementation phases.

Key Action Items

  • Review existing grant applications or planned applications to the Highways to Boulevards Program for potential adjustments
  • Monitor for updated program guidelines from Caltrans reflecting the new funding allocation formula
  • Assess local projects that might benefit from the increased flexibility in funding allocation
  • Consider whether projects previously deemed unfeasible due to funding constraints might now be viable with the more flexible allocation
  • Prepare for potential competitive advantage by developing strong implementation plans that could benefit from increased implementation funding

Additional Requirements

  • Adjust planning for highway conversion projects to account for the more flexible funding allocation

  • Maintain partnerships with Caltrans as required for program participation

Summary:
This legislative change amends the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to allow lead applicant agencies to request letters of no prejudice from the Secretary of Transportation. This would permit agencies to use their own funds for approved port infrastructure projects with the ability to receive reimbursement from program funds later, provided certain conditions are met. The provisions sunset on June 30, 2033, and are repealed on January 1, 2034.

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

County: Counties that operate ports or serve as lead applicants for port infrastructure projects can now apply for letters of no prejudice, allowing them to advance projects using their own funds with potential future reimbursement from state program funds. Joint Powers Authority: JPAs involved in port operations or infrastructure that serve as lead applicants for port projects can now apply for letters of no prejudice, allowing them to advance projects using their own funds with potential future reimbursement from state program funds.

Key Action Items

  • Assess agency financial capacity to front costs for eligible projects before state reimbursement
  • Develop procedures for applying for letters of no prejudice from the Secretary of Transportation
  • Create tracking systems for expenditures that may qualify for future reimbursement
  • Update capital improvement plans to reflect potential for expedited project implementation

Deadlines

  • 2034-01-01: Letter of no prejudice provisions are repealed (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

This $100 million appropriation increases available funding for eligible county-sponsored projects. (Deadline: June 30, 2027) Special District: Special districts with transportation, recreation, or public works responsibilities may be eligible to apply for ATP funding for active transportation infrastructure within their jurisdictions. This includes transit districts, park districts, and other special purpose entities. (Deadline: June 30, 2027) School District: School districts may partner with cities or counties on Safe Routes to School projects funded through the ATP, improving walking and biking infrastructure around schools. (Deadline: June 30, 2027) Joint Powers Authority: JPAs formed for transportation, trail management, or regional planning purposes may be eligible to apply for ATP funding for multi-jurisdictional active transportation projects.
