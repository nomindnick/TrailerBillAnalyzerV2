Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SECTION 1.

Summary:
This bill amends Government Code Section 8594.14 to revise requirements for law enforcement agencies requesting Ebony Alerts for missing Black youth. It expands determinations agencies must make before requesting an alert, including requiring they've exhausted local resources and have information that could help in recovery if shared publicly. It also makes CHP activation mandatory (rather than discretionary) when they concur with an agency's determination and requires CHP to submit a report by July 1, 2027.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District

Impact Description:
City: City police departments must follow revised, more stringent requirements when determining whether to request an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth. This includes demonstrating they've used all available local resources and have information that could assist in recovery if shared with the public. County: County sheriff's departments must follow revised, more stringent requirements when determining whether to request an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth. This includes demonstrating they've used all available local resources and have information that could assist in recovery if shared with the public. Special District: Special districts with their own law enforcement agencies (such as transit police, school police, etc.) must follow revised requirements when determining whether to request an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth.

Key Action Items

  • Update law enforcement agency protocols for handling missing persons cases involving Black youth
  • Train law enforcement personnel on the revised determination requirements for requesting Ebony Alerts
  • Review and update any written policies or manuals that address missing persons procedures

Additional Requirements

  • Law enforcement agencies must demonstrate they've used all available local resources before requesting an Ebony Alert
  • Agencies must verify they have information that could assist in recovery if shared with the public
  • Agencies must understand that CHP is now required (not just authorized) to activate alerts when they concur with the agency's determination

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - This practice group serves cities, counties, and special districts that operate law enforcement agencies directly affected by these changes to Ebony Alert requirements.
  • Governance (secondary) - The changes affect operational policies and procedures that govern how local law enforcement agencies handle missing persons cases involving Black youth.
  • Litigation (secondary) - There could potentially be litigation resulting from disputes about whether law enforcement agencies properly followed the new determination requirements in particular cases.
