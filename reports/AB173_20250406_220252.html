Trailer Bill Sections: SECTION 1.

Summary:
This legislation amends Government Code Section 8594.14 to modify the Ebony Alert system for missing Black youth. It expands and revises the criteria law enforcement agencies must meet before requesting an alert, makes CHP activation mandatory when criteria are met, and requires a report to the Governor and Legislature by July 1, 2027. This impacts local law enforcement agencies operated by cities and counties.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County

Impact Description:
City: City police departments must follow revised and expanded criteria when determining whether to request an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth. The law now requires specific determinations including that all available local resources have been used and that public dissemination of information could assist in recovery. County: County sheriff departments must follow revised and expanded criteria when determining whether to request an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth. The law now requires specific determinations including that all available local resources have been used and that public dissemination of information could assist in recovery.

Key Action Items

  • Review and update missing persons policies and procedures
  • Develop clear guidelines for determining when to request an Ebony Alert under the new criteria
  • Train law enforcement personnel on the revised requirements
  • Establish coordination protocols with CHP regarding mandatory alert activation
  • Implement documentation processes to demonstrate compliance with the new determination requirements

Additional Requirements

  • Law enforcement agencies must demonstrate they have used all available local resources before requesting an Ebony Alert
  • Agencies must determine that information is available that could assist in safe recovery if disseminated to the public
  • Agencies must comply with any additional determination requirements specified in the amended Government Code Section 8594.14

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - Directly relevant to city and county law enforcement agencies that must implement the new Ebony Alert request criteria
  • Governance (secondary) - Relates to policy changes affecting public agency operations and interagency coordination
  • Litigation (secondary) - Potential liability concerns related to implementation of the revised missing persons protocols

The Transportation Agency is authorized to prioritize and allocate funding to projects at risk of losing federal/local funding commitments or facing schedule delays. This affects local agencies that sponsor, manage, or co-fund grade separation projects.

Summary:
This bill modifies the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to authorize lead applicant agencies to apply for a 'letter of no prejudice' that allows them to spend their own funds on approved port infrastructure projects with the possibility of future reimbursement from program funds. This creates a new financing option for local agencies involved in port and freight infrastructure projects, while establishing a sunset date of June 30, 2033 for these provisions.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • Special District
  • City
  • County
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
Special District: Port authorities, harbor districts, and other special districts that operate or administer port facilities now have the option to request a letter of no prejudice to advance fund their port infrastructure projects and receive later reimbursement from state program funds. This provides greater flexibility to move forward with critical projects without waiting for state funding disbursement. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) City: Cities with ports or that serve as lead applicant agencies for port-related roadway infrastructure projects can utilize the letter of no prejudice mechanism to advance projects using local funds with the potential for future state reimbursement. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) County: Counties that administer transportation infrastructure projects serving ports can utilize the letter of no prejudice mechanism to advance projects using county funds with potential for future state reimbursement. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) Joint Powers Authority: JPAs formed to administer port or freight infrastructure projects can now utilize the letter of no prejudice mechanism to advance fund approved projects and seek later reimbursement from state program funds. (Deadline: June 30, 2033)

Key Action Items

  • Develop internal procedures for requesting letters of no prejudice from the Secretary of Transportation
  • Create accounting systems to track expenditures eligible for future reimbursement
  • Review existing port and freight infrastructure projects to identify candidates for this financing approach
  • Update financial planning to potentially leverage local funds with the assurance of future state reimbursement
  • Ensure compliance with any conditions specified in the letter of no prejudice

Deadlines

  • 2033-06-30: Sunset date - provisions authorizing letters of no prejudice become inoperative (Affected agencies: Special District, City, County, Joint Powers Authority)
  • 2034-01-01: Repeal date - provisions are repealed from law (Affected agencies: Special District, City, County, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Lead applicant agencies must apply to the Secretary of Transportation or designee for a letter of no prejudice
  • The project or component must be included in the program of projects awarded by the Transportation Agency
  • Expenditures must meet eligibility requirements to qualify for future reimbursement
  • Local agencies must have capacity to advance fund projects before state reimbursement
  • Agencies must maintain proper documentation of expenditures and project approval status

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Public Finance (primary) - The letter of no prejudice is fundamentally a public financing mechanism allowing local agencies to advance fund projects with assurance of future reimbursement, requiring expertise in public agency financing.
  • Facilities and Business (secondary) - Relates to infrastructure development projects and business operations for agencies involved in port facilities and transportation infrastructure.
