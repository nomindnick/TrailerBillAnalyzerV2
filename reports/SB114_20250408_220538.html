Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 2., SEC. 4., SEC. 5., SEC. 22., SEC. 50.

Summary:
This bill revises the funding formula for county offices of education by increasing the base grant component of the alternative education grant, modifying average daily attendance calculations, and establishing additional funding for juvenile court schools and county community schools. It also creates a new Student Support and Enrichment Block Grant providing $3,000 per unit of average daily attendance. These changes begin with the 2023-24 fiscal year and will significantly impact county office of education funding, while indirectly affecting school districts through attendance accounting.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • County Office of Education
  • School District

Impact Description:
County Office of Education: Increases funding through revised alternative education grant calculations, including higher base grant amounts and $200,000 add-ons for operating juvenile court schools and county community schools. Establishes a new Student Support and Enrichment Block Grant of $3,000 per unit of average daily attendance. (Deadline: July 01, 2023) School District: Attendance for pupils not funded under specified provisions who enroll in county-operated schools will be credited to their district of residence, potentially affecting district funding calculations. (Deadline: July 01, 2023)

Key Action Items

  • County offices of education must incorporate new funding into budget planning for 2023-24 fiscal year
  • County offices of education must update local control and accountability plans to reflect the Student Support and Enrichment Block Grant
  • County offices and school districts must coordinate on attendance accounting processes
  • County offices that operate juvenile court schools or county community schools should document qualification for the $200,000 add-ons

Deadlines

  • 2023-07-01: Implementation of revised funding formula for 2023-24 fiscal year (Affected agencies: County Office of Education, School District)

Additional Requirements

  • Update budget planning processes to account for revised funding formula
  • Incorporate Student Support and Enrichment Block Grant into local control and accountability plans
  • Implement revised attendance accounting procedures for alternative education programs
  • Track eligibility for juvenile court school and county community school add-on funding

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Facilities and Business (primary) - This legislation directly impacts county office of education budget development, financial planning, and resource allocation due to significant funding formula changes.
  • Public Finance (primary) - The bill substantially revises public education financing mechanisms, modifying the local control funding formula for county offices of education.
  • Governance (secondary) - County boards of education will need to understand these funding changes when making budget decisions and approving local control and accountability plans.
  • Student (secondary) - The Student Support and Enrichment Block Grant is intended to fund student services and programs, which would fall under this practice area.

  • County Office of Education
  • Charter School

County Office of Education: Alters how COEs receive reimbursement for school meals provided in county-operated schools, potentially changing fiscal planning and accounting procedures. Charter School: Explicitly includes charter schools in the state meal reimbursement program, changing how meal services are funded and potentially affecting budget planning.

Key Action Items

  • Review current meal program accounting practices to align with new reimbursement model
  • Ensure continued compliance with federal School Breakfast Program and National School Lunch Program requirements
  • Update budget projections based on the new reimbursement approach
  • Train food service and business office staff on new reimbursement procedures
  • Assess potential fiscal impact of the change from 'all nonreimbursed expenses' to specifically 'reduced-price and paid meals'

Additional Requirements

  • Maintain participation in federal School Breakfast Program and National School Lunch Program
  • Comply with all applicable state laws and regulations regarding school meals
  • Properly document and account for reduced-price and paid meals served to pupils
  • Continue providing two free meals per schoolday to any pupil who requests them

Relevant Practice Groups
