Summary:
The bill revises California's public school financing system by increasing the alternative education grant's base component, modifying how average daily attendance is calculated, and establishing new funding add-ons and a Student Support and Enrichment Block Grant. These changes affect local agencies involved in K-12 education funding, both directly and indirectly, with implementation beginning in the 2023–24 fiscal year.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • School District
  • Charter School
  • County Office of Education

Impact Description:
School District: Changes to the alternative education grant—specifically the increased base grant and revised average daily attendance calculations—affect state funding allocations to school districts. School districts will need to update their reporting systems and budgeting models to comply with the new formulas. (Deadline: July 01, 2023) Charter School: As state funding for charter schools is determined using the adjusted local control funding formula, charter schools will be indirectly affected by the revised calculations for average daily attendance. They must review their compliance and funding reporting processes. (Deadline: July 01, 2023) County Office of Education: County offices of education will receive additional funding through $200,000 add-ons for operating juvenile court schools and county community schools, as well as a Student Support and Enrichment Block Grant of $3,000 per unit of average daily attendance. Agencies must verify program eligibility and integrate these allocations into local control plans. (Deadline: July 01, 2023)

Key Action Items

  • Review and update attendance calculation and reporting systems to ensure compliance with revised funding formulas.
  • Conduct internal reviews to confirm eligibility for additional add-on funding for juvenile court and community schools.
  • Revise budgeting models to accommodate the increased base grant and the new Student Support and Enrichment Block Grant.
  • Update local control and accountability plans to reflect the new alternative education grant provisions.

Deadlines

  • 2023-07-01: Effective start of the 2023–24 fiscal year; agencies must implement the updated funding formulas and reporting procedures. (Affected agencies: School District, Charter School, County Office of Education)

Additional Requirements

  • Update attendance calculation methods as stipulated in the revised legislation.
  • Integrate new funding allocation processes into existing financial and operational systems.
  • Ensure that local control and accountability plans reflect all changes to alternative education grant formulas and new support block grants.

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Public Finance (primary) - The legislative change directly alters funding calculations and grant allocations, necessitating expertise in public finance analysis and compliance.
  • Charter Schools (primary) - Charter schools are directly impacted by the changes in funding formulas, requiring targeted legal guidance to ensure proper compliance and reporting.

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 13., SEC. 14.

Summary:
The legislative change amends the California School Finance Authority Act by revising how local intercepts are handled for financing projects. It replaces the previous written notice requirement to the Controller with a process where participating parties submit a request to the county treasurer (or appropriate fiscal officer) to have the county participate. In addition, it limits school districts and county offices of education—previously able to intercept payments on short-term financings—to doing so only for indebtedness where repayment is deemed probable. These changes impose new compliance and procedural requirements at the local level, affecting how funding is managed and requiring updates to internal processes.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • School District
  • County Office of Education
  • County

Impact Description:
School District: School districts must now assess and confirm that the repayment of bonds is probable before intercepting payments. They are also required to initiate participation by sending a request to the county treasurer or fiscal officer rather than providing written notice to the Controller. County Office of Education: County offices of education, similar to school districts, face a restriction on intercepting payments only for cases with probable repayment. They must coordinate with county fiscal officers for the local intercept process. County: Counties are now directly involved as they are required to process requests from participating parties. If a county agrees to participate, its treasurer or fiscal officer must perform an apportionment or revenue transfer, necessitating updates to internal revenue transfer and compliance procedures.

Key Action Items

  • Local agencies should review and update financial procedures to align with the new probable repayment criteria.
  • Counties must develop and implement processes for receiving and processing local intercept participation requests.
  • School districts and county offices of education should adjust board approval protocols to reflect the updated participation election process.

Additional Requirements

  • Implement a process to submit local intercept requests via county fiscal officers
  • Verify that funding interceptions apply only when repayment is deemed probable
  • Update revenue transfer and apportionment procedures at the county level
  • Revise board resolution processes to comply with the new participation election requirements

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Public Finance (primary) - The change directly affects bond financing mechanisms, credit enhancements, and revenue transfers, all of which are central to public finance operations.
  • Municipal (secondary) - Local government entities, particularly counties, must adjust their operational processes to comply with the new revenue transfer and participation request protocols.
  • Governance (secondary) - The requirement for formal board actions to elect participation impacts the governance operations of school districts and county offices of education.
