Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 2.

Summary:
The legislative change directs the Secretary of Transportation to work with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission to identify and reallocate available state funds for grade separation projects that risk losing federal and local funding. Although the bill primarily targets state-level transportation agencies, its provisions will indirectly affect local public agencies that participate in funding and executing these projects. Local agencies involved in infrastructure projects—such as cities, counties, special districts, and joint powers authorities—may need to review their funding commitments, adjust project plans, and coordinate more closely with state agencies to offset potential shortfalls.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities with grade separation projects that rely partly on local funding commitments may see state reallocation of funds affect their financial planning and project timelines. They should review ongoing projects to ensure that local funding gaps are addressed. County: Counties involved in transportation infrastructure projects may be impacted if state funds are directed to cover local funding shortfalls in grade separation projects. They need to verify that their matching funds and project schedules align with new state procedures. Special District: Special districts that manage specific transportation or infrastructure projects may face operational changes if state funds are reallocated to support projects at risk. They should prepare for compliance with revised funding allocation measures. Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities formed to manage multi-agency transportation projects might be affected by state prioritization and direct allocation of funds. Coordinated efforts will be necessary to ensure that local funding commitments are met and projects remain on schedule.

Key Action Items

  • Local agencies should audit their grade separation project funding to identify any gaps in federal or local funding commitments.
  • Establish or enhance communication channels with state transportation agencies to understand state reallocation priorities.
  • Review and update internal procedures to ensure compliance with new funding and reporting requirements.
  • Prepare documentation on project timelines and funding vulnerabilities for potential state intervention.

Deadlines

  • 2025-04-30: Deadline for the Secretary of Transportation to report to the Legislature on identified funding for grade separation projects, which local agencies should monitor for detailed guidance on funding reallocations. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Review and adjust local funding commitments for grade separation projects
  • Coordinate with state agencies on project prioritization and funding reallocation
  • Document project schedules and funding vulnerabilities
  • Prepare for compliance with revised reporting and funding allocation processes

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - Cities, counties, and special districts are directly involved in local infrastructure projects and will need legal guidance on navigating changes in funding and compliance processes.
  • Public Finance (primary) - The reallocation of state funds for grade separation projects affects local financial planning and matching fund requirements, making expertise in public finance critical.
  • Facilities and Business (secondary) - Local agencies must adapt their capital improvement strategies and business operations in response to new funding mechanisms and project prioritization measures.
  • Governance (secondary) - The state-mandated coordination, reporting, and compliance requirements call for adjustments in local governance and oversight processes.

Summary:
The legislative change expands the Transportation Agency’s accountability program to include funds from the Greenhouse Gas Reduction Fund for the formula-based component of the Transit and Intercity Rail Capital Program. In practical terms, regional transportation planning agencies – which are often structured as cities, counties, special districts, or joint powers authorities – must now update and submit their regional short‐term financial plans and transit operator data on an annual basis (starting with the 2025–26 fiscal year and continuing in the following fiscal years) to qualify for funding. The shift in funding source and reporting requirements imposes new compliance and administrative tasks, indirectly affecting local public agencies involved in transit planning and operations.

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

This change could require adjustments to internal reporting processes and coordination with state authorities to secure fund allocations. County: Counties serving as regional transportation planning agencies must update their regional short‐term financial plans for the 2025–26 fiscal year and provide updated transit operator data in subsequent years. This will likely demand enhancements in financial monitoring and reporting systems to meet new state-imposed standards. Special District: Transit-oriented special districts acting as regional transportation planning agencies are subject to additional reporting requirements to access new funding sources. They must adjust financial data submissions and monitor state-mandated guideline modifications. Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities that help coordinate regional transit initiatives are required to update and submit the relevant financial plans and transit operator data. This necessitates enhanced coordination among participating entities to comply with the new submission timelines.

  • Establish a monitoring process for guideline modification deadlines announced by the Transportation Agency.
  • Engage legal counsel and relevant department heads to ensure full compliance with the expanded accountability program.

Deadlines

  • N/A: Submission of updated transit operator data for the 2026–27 and 2027–28 fiscal years as mandated under the modified guidelines. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Submit updated transit operator data for FY 2025–26, FY 2026–27, and FY 2027–28

Summary:
This legislative change amends state law governing the High-Speed Rail Authority by expanding the Inspector General's powers. The amendments require that all contracts and contract amendments using high-speed rail project funds executed on or after July 1, 2024, include specific provisions granting the Inspector General access to records and property. In addition, whistleblower protections are extended to employees of contractors receiving such project funds. While the change directly targets the High-Speed Rail Authority and its Inspector General, it indirectly affects local public agencies—especially those acting as contracting entities—by imposing new compliance, contractual, and oversight requirements.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

This exposes them to increased audit and oversight activities. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) County: Counties participating in high-speed rail initiatives or receiving related funds are required to revise contract terms to include specific IG oversight provisions and adapt internal policies to extend whistleblower protections, leading to enhanced compliance obligations. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Special District: Special Districts involved in transportation or infrastructure projects funded by high-speed rail project funds must amend their contracts to include the required IG access provisions and adjust policies to comply with enhanced whistleblower protections. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities formed by multiple local agencies that enter into high-speed rail-related contracts must update contract templates to include provisions for IG record access and extend whistleblower protections, thereby increasing their compliance responsibilities. (Deadline: July 01, 2024)

Key Action Items

  • Review and update all contract templates to incorporate the mandated inspector general access provisions for contracts executed on or after July 1, 2024.
  • Revise internal compliance policies to extend whistleblower protection measures to contractor employees.
  • Consult with legal counsel specializing in Municipal and Public Finance matters to ensure full adherence to the new oversight and contractual requirements.
  • Establish procedures for monitoring notifications from the Department of Finance regarding changes to the Inspector General’s proposed budget.

Deadlines

  • 2024-07-01: All contracts and contract amendments using high-speed rail project funds executed on or after this date must include the specified inspector general access provisions. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • All new contracts must include provisions granting the Inspector General full access to records and property related to high-speed rail project activities.
  • Contractor employees must be provided with extended whistleblower protections.
  • Affected local agencies need to update compliance policies and contractual agreements to meet these new oversight requirements.

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 13.

Summary:
The legislative change amends the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to allow a lead applicant agency to apply for a letter of no prejudice from the Secretary of Transportation. This letter permits the agency to expend its own funds on eligible port and freight projects with the expectation of future reimbursement if specific conditions are met. The change introduces new funding flexibility as well as compliance and reporting obligations. Its impact is indirect on local agencies that administer or operate these projects, affecting budgeting, financial planning, and oversight. The provisions are temporary, becoming inoperative on 2033-06-30 and repealed on 2034-01-01.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities acting as lead applicant agencies for port-related projects can now apply for a letter of no prejudice to use local funds upfront, subject to later reimbursement. This change affects budget planning and requires adherence to new compliance and reporting conditions. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) County: Counties that manage or operate eligible port infrastructure projects may benefit from the new mechanism that enables them to utilize local funds in advance, impacting funding strategies and necessitating proper documentation for reimbursement. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) Special District: Special Districts involved in port-specific projects can now access the option to expend their own funds, thereby affecting their internal budgeting processes and obligating them to meet compliance requirements to secure future reimbursement. (Deadline: June 30, 2033) Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities coordinating port projects among multiple public agencies must adhere to the new funding provisions. This means collaborative application efforts, shared financial oversight, and coordinated reporting to satisfy reimbursement conditions. (Deadline: June 30, 2033)

Key Action Items

  • Assess eligibility for the letter of no prejudice funding mechanism
  • Develop and submit a comprehensive application to the Secretary of Transportation
  • Align project budgets and financial documentation with new reimbursement conditions
  • Implement internal compliance measures to track eligible expenditures
  • Monitor legislative deadlines (2033-06-30 and 2034-01-01) to adjust project timelines accordingly

Deadlines

  • 2033-06-30: The letter of no prejudice provisions become inoperative after this date, so agencies must apply before then to take advantage of the funding mechanism. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)
  • 2034-01-01: The provisions are fully repealed as of this date, ending the option to expend local funds under the new mechanism. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Ensure comprehensive documentation of eligible project expenditures for future reimbursement
