Trailer Bill Sections: SECTION 1.

Summary:
This legislative change amends Government Code Section 8594.14 regarding the Ebony Alert system for missing Black youth. It expands and revises the determinations law enforcement agencies must make before requesting an Ebony Alert from the California Highway Patrol (CHP), changes the CHP's activation from discretionary to mandatory when they concur with the agency's determination, and requires the CHP to submit a report by July 1, 2027. This primarily impacts local law enforcement agencies operated by cities and counties.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County

Impact Description:
City: City police departments must follow expanded criteria when requesting Ebony Alerts, including demonstrating they've used all available local resources and that public dissemination of information could assist in recovery of the missing person. County: County sheriff's departments must follow expanded criteria when requesting Ebony Alerts, including demonstrating they've used all available local resources and that public dissemination of information could assist in recovery of the missing person.

Key Action Items

  • Review and update policies and procedures for requesting Ebony Alerts
  • Develop documentation systems to demonstrate use of all available local resources
  • Train relevant law enforcement personnel on the revised Ebony Alert criteria
  • Establish protocols for determining when information dissemination would assist in recovery

Additional Requirements

  • Update missing persons policies to incorporate expanded Ebony Alert criteria
  • Ensure law enforcement agencies can document exhaustion of local resources
  • Develop criteria for determining when public information dissemination would be helpful

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - This practice group advises cities and counties, whose law enforcement agencies are directly impacted by the changes to Ebony Alert requirements
  • Governance (secondary) - Changes may require updates to internal policies and procedures for handling missing persons cases
  • Labor and Employment (secondary) - May require training updates for law enforcement personnel on new Ebony Alert request requirements

Summary:
This bill modifies the Reconnecting Communities: Highways to Boulevards Pilot Program by changing the funding allocation structure. Previously, the program mandated a fixed 25% for planning and 75% for implementation. The amendment changes this to 'up to 25%' for planning and the remainder for implementation, giving Caltrans more flexibility in fund allocation. This impacts local agencies that may apply for grants to convert underutilized state highways into multimodal corridors serving underserved communities.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities that apply for grants under this program will be affected by the revised funding allocation. The change from a fixed 25% for planning to 'up to 25%' means potentially more funding could be directed toward implementation projects. Cities may need to adjust their grant application strategies and project timelines accordingly. County: Counties involved in highway conversion projects may see shifts in available planning vs. implementation funding. This could affect county transportation planning and project execution strategies for converting state highways within county jurisdiction. Special District: Transportation-related special districts eligible for this program may experience changes in how they can access funding between planning and implementation phases, potentially favoring districts ready for implementation over those in planning stages. Joint Powers Authority: JPAs formed for regional transportation initiatives will be affected by the modified funding allocation, particularly those focused on highway conversions in underserved communities.

Key Action Items

  • Monitor Caltrans announcements regarding implementation of the modified funding allocation
  • Review and adjust current and planned grant applications to align with potential shifts toward implementation funding
  • Evaluate project readiness to potentially capitalize on increased implementation funding
  • Identify opportunities for accelerating planning phases to be ready for implementation funding

Additional Requirements

  • Stay informed of updated program guidelines from Caltrans
  • Assess current highway conversion projects for alignment with potential funding shifts
  • Prepare alternative funding strategies for planning if less funding becomes available for that phase

Relevant Practice Groups

This bill amends the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to allow lead applicant agencies to apply for a letter of no prejudice from the Secretary of Transportation. This mechanism permits agencies to use their own funds for approved port infrastructure projects with the ability to receive future reimbursement from program funds, subject to certain conditions. The provision expires on June 30, 2033.

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities that operate ports or manage port-adjacent infrastructure (such as Long Beach, Los Angeles, Oakland, San Francisco, San Diego) can now initiate approved port and freight infrastructure projects using local funds and seek reimbursement later through the program. This provides timing flexibility for priority projects. County: Counties involved in regional transportation planning that includes port and freight infrastructure can now use local funds for approved projects with potential for later reimbursement. Special District: Port authorities and transportation special districts can advance approved projects using their own funds with the ability to receive reimbursement later through the program. Joint Powers Authority: Regional transportation JPAs involved in port and freight infrastructure projects can utilize this mechanism to advance approved projects before state funding is available.

Key Action Items

  • Identify eligible port and freight infrastructure projects in approved program
  • Develop internal procedures for applying for letters of no prejudice
  • Assess financial capacity to advance funds for projects before reimbursement
  • Create accounting procedures to track expenditures eligible for reimbursement
  • Update capital improvement plans to potentially accelerate port/freight projects

Deadlines

Additional Requirements
