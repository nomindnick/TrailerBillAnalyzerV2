Trailer Bill Sections: SECTION 1.

Summary:
This legislation amends the Ebony Alert system for missing Black youth by expanding and revising the criteria law enforcement agencies must meet before requesting an alert from the California Highway Patrol (CHP). It changes CHP's role from discretionary to mandatory activation when criteria are met, and requires the CHP to submit a report by July 1, 2027. This impacts local law enforcement agencies operated by cities and counties.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County

Impact Description:
City: City police departments must follow revised criteria when requesting Ebony Alerts, including demonstrating they've exhausted all available local resources and that public information dissemination could assist in recovery of the missing person. The change makes CHP activation mandatory when they concur with the local agency's determination. County: County sheriff departments must follow revised criteria when requesting Ebony Alerts, including demonstrating they've exhausted all available local resources and that public information dissemination could assist in recovery of the missing person. The change makes CHP activation mandatory when they concur with the local agency's determination.

Key Action Items

  • Review and update internal policies regarding missing persons cases involving Black youth
  • Train law enforcement personnel on the revised Ebony Alert request criteria
  • Develop documentation protocols to demonstrate compliance with 'all available local resources' requirement
  • Establish procedures to determine when information would assist in recovery if disseminated to the public

Additional Requirements

  • Law enforcement agencies must demonstrate they have used all available local resources before requesting an Ebony Alert
  • Agencies must determine that information exists which, if disseminated to the public, could assist in the safe recovery of the missing person
  • Agencies must meet all other determination requirements specified in the revised Government Code Section 8594.14

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - This practice group advises cities and counties that operate law enforcement agencies directly affected by the changes to Ebony Alert requirements.
  • Governance (secondary) - This practice group may advise local agency governing boards on policy updates needed to comply with the revised Ebony Alert procedures.
  • Litigation (secondary) - Failure to properly apply the new Ebony Alert criteria in missing persons cases could potentially lead to liability concerns.
