Summary:
The bill amends the definition of 'month' for calculating benefits under the Classified Community College Employee Summer Assistance Program by setting it as 20 days or 4 weeks of 5 days each (including legal holidays). This change impacts participating Community College districts by requiring them to update payroll systems, eligibility criteria, and related employee communication materials.

Impact Description:
Community College: Community College districts that participate in the Summer Assistance Program must update their payroll and HR systems to reflect the new definition of a 'month'. This will affect how classified employees’ eligibility is calculated (i.e., determining if they work 11 months or fewer out of a 12-month period) and require revisions to internal policies and employee notifications.

Key Action Items

  • Review current payroll and HR systems to determine necessary updates for the new month definition.
  • Develop and distribute revised policy guidelines and employee communications regarding eligibility calculations under the updated definition.

Additional Requirements

  • Update payroll systems and software parameters
  • Revise and communicate changes in eligibility requirements
  • Conduct internal audits to ensure compliance with the new definition

Summary:
This legislative change expands the Part‐time Community College Faculty Health Insurance Program by extending coverage to multidistrict part‐time faculty. Community college districts are now required to update their health insurance programs to include faculty teaching at two or more community colleges, implement reimbursement procedures when such faculty individually purchase insurance, and meet specific criteria in order to receive full funding apportionments from the Chancellor by June 15 each year.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • Community College

Impact Description:
Community College: Community college districts must modify their existing health insurance programs to include multidistrict part‐time faculty. They must establish new reimbursement mechanisms for faculty who self-purchase health insurance and ensure the program meets criteria such as offering full-time equivalent benefits and limiting premium differences to qualify for additional state funding. (Deadline: June 15, 2024)

Key Action Items

  • Review current health insurance policies and update them to include multidistrict part-time faculty.
  • Develop and implement a detailed reimbursement system for cases where faculty purchase their own health insurance.
  • Adjust administrative processes to ensure the program meets all statutory criteria (equal benefits, premium limits, full coverage) to qualify for enhanced funding.
  • Coordinate with the Chancellor's office to verify funding apportionment procedures and deadlines.

Deadlines

  • 2024-06-15: Annual deadline for the Chancellor of the California Community Colleges to apportion remaining funds to community college districts that meet the required criteria. (Affected agencies: Community College)

Additional Requirements

  • Include multidistrict part-time faculty in the health insurance program
  • Reimburse multidistrict part-time faculty for the district's proportionate share of individually purchased insurance premiums
  • Offer part-time faculty the same benefits as full-time faculty
  • Limit individual premiums to be equivalent to those paid by full-time faculty
  • Meet specified statutory criteria to qualify for full funding apportionments

Relevant Practice Groups

Summary:
The California Student Housing Revolving Loan Fund Act of 2022 creates a new state funding mechanism to provide zero‐interest loans for constructing affordable student and faculty/staff housing at eligible institutions. While the primary targets are higher education systems (University of California and California State University) and the California Community Colleges – the latter qualifying as local public agencies – the changes also have indirect implications for cities, counties, and special districts that oversee local planning, zoning, and infrastructure development around campuses. In addition, the legislation mandates a report submission by the California School Finance Authority and the California Educational Facilities Authority by March 15, 2025, which may affect local agencies by informing future regulatory and funding practices.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • Community College
  • City
  • County
  • Special District

Impact Description:
Community College: Community Colleges (typically organized as community college districts) are directly eligible to apply for zero‐interest loans to finance affordable student and faculty/staff housing projects. They must evaluate potential construction or refurbishment projects and adhere to the guidelines of the new revolving loan fund. City: Cities may experience increased housing development near local higher education campuses as a result of new constructions financed through the loan fund. This could necessitate updates to local zoning ordinances, permitting processes, and infrastructure planning. County: Counties could see changes in service delivery demands and infrastructure needs from expanded student and staff housing developments around campus areas. Local planning departments may need to work closely with affected colleges and municipalities. Special District: Special Districts responsible for utilities and related services might face adjustments due to increased loads or infrastructural changes stemming from new student/faculty housing projects. Close coordination with local agencies and project developers will be crucial.

Key Action Items

  • Community Colleges should review the loan application guidelines and assess potential housing projects for funding eligibility.
  • Local government agencies (Cities, Counties, and Special Districts) must examine current zoning ordinances and planning processes to accommodate potential new housing developments.
  • Agencies should establish communication channels with state authorities to monitor compliance requirements and reporting deadlines.
  • Practice groups should prepare advisory notes on bond issuance, loan compliance, and facility planning associated with the new funding mechanism.
  • Stakeholders need to coordinate interdisciplinary efforts to align project proposals with state funding mandates and local regulatory frameworks.

Deadlines

  • 2025-03-15: Deadline for the California School Finance Authority and the California Educational Facilities Authority to submit a comprehensive report on the implementation and impact of the California Student Housing Revolving Loan Fund Act of 2022. (Affected agencies: Community College, City, County, Special District)

Additional Requirements

  • Assess project viability under new fund requirements
  • Ensure compliance with state and local regulations in housing construction projects
  • Update internal procedural and financial protocols to align with appropriations and reporting mandates
  • Coordinate with local planning and zoning boards to support housing development needs

Relevant Practice Groups

Summary:
The bill amends the appeal process under the Cal Grant program by allowing, for the 2023–24 academic year only, eligible institutions that have failed to meet the 3‐year cohort default rate due to the acquisition of an out-of-state institution (which later closed between the 2019–20 and 2022–23 academic years) to file an appeal. This adjustment can help affected institutions retain their eligibility for Cal Grant awards by considering unique circumstances that previously resulted in disqualification.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • Community College

Impact Description:
Community College: Community Colleges that participate in the Cal Grant programs—such as those offering the Expanded Entitlement and Transfer Entitlement awards—may now pursue an appeal if an out-of-state acquisition adversely affected their 3-year cohort default rate. Affected institutions must compile documentation to show the acquisition’s impact and the closure of the acquired institution within the specified timeframe.

Key Action Items

  • Review current cohort default rate data and assess if the out-of-state acquisition factor impacted eligibility.
  • Prepare comprehensive documentation outlining the acquisition details and confirm closure dates of the acquired institution.
  • Engage with legal counsel and compliance teams to ensure the appeal submission meets the new criteria for the 2023–24 academic year.

Deadlines

  • N/A: The new appeal provision applies exclusively to the 2023–24 academic year. Affected institutions should establish internal timelines to ensure their appeal is properly documented and submitted within the relevant academic period. (Affected agencies: Community College)

Additional Requirements

  • Verify that the acquisition of the out-of-state institution meets the stipulated criteria (including closure between 2019–20 and 2022–23).
  • Document the impact on the institution’s 3-year cohort default rate and prepare the necessary appeal documentation for submission.

Relevant Practice Groups

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 5., SEC. 6., SEC. 7., SEC. 8.

Summary:
The legislative change revises the NextUp program's eligibility criteria for foster youth in California Community Colleges. Instead of requiring that students be under 25 at the start of every academic year in which they participate, eligibility is now determined solely based on the student's age at the commencement of the academic year in which they first enroll. Additionally, the law eliminates the provision requiring income waiver conditions. As a result, affected community colleges will need to update their enrollment policies, internal regulations, and communications with prospective students.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • Community College

Impact Description:
Community College: Community colleges must modify their NextUp program policies to reflect that foster youth eligibility is now based on age at first enrollment and that income criteria waiver is no longer required. This will likely necessitate revisions to enrollment procedures, internal compliance systems, and communication with program staff and prospective students.

Key Action Items

  • Update NextUp enrollment guidelines to reflect the new age criterion at first enrollment
  • Eliminate processes related to income waiver requirements in program documentation
  • Revise internal regulations and compliance manuals
  • Train program staff on the changes and communicate new eligibility standards to prospective foster youth

Additional Requirements

  • Amend enrollment eligibility criteria to calculate age based on the academic year at first enrollment
  • Remove income waiver procedures from the NextUp program application and review process

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Student (primary) - Since the eligibility criteria directly affect how foster youth are admitted into the NextUp program, changes will impact student enrollment processes and student rights within community colleges.
  • Governance (primary) - Boards of Governors and community college administrations must update their regulatory frameworks and oversight protocols to ensure compliance with the new statutory requirements.
