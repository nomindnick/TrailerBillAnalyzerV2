Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 2.

Summary:
The legislative change requires the Secretary of Transportation to work with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission to identify and reallocate available state transportation funds toward grade separation projects. While the measure primarily targets state-level funding and project management, local agencies may be indirectly affected because many such projects involve local funding commitments and collaborative planning. Cities, Counties, Special Districts, and Joint Powers Authorities may need to adjust project timelines, reassess local funding arrangements, and enhance coordination with state agencies.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities with grade separation projects may have to reexamine local funding contributions and adjust project timelines if state funds are reallocated to cover shortfalls. They will also need to coordinate with state transportation agencies to align local project requirements with the new funding priorities. County: Counties involved in local transportation initiatives could see changes in funding arrangements as the state reassigns resources to cover grade separation projects at risk. They should reassess local funding structures and adjust project implementation timelines in line with state priorities. Special District: Transportation-related special districts may be impacted by the reallocation of state funds, which could affect the scheduling and operation of local projects. They will need to comply with new funding allocation procedures and possibly revise their project planning to align with state priorities. Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities managing multi-agency transportation projects may face indirect impacts due to changes in state funding allocations. Enhanced inter-agency collaboration and an adjustment of project timelines may be necessary to secure and integrate allocated funds.

Key Action Items

  • Review local grade separation projects to assess exposure to potential funding shortfalls.
  • Establish or update communication protocols with state transportation agencies regarding funding reallocations.
  • Reevaluate and adjust local funding commitments and project timelines in line with state priorities.

Deadlines

  • 2025-04-30: Deadline for the Secretary of Transportation to report to the Legislature on the identification of available state transportation funding for impacted grade separation projects. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Coordinate with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission to identify available funding.
  • Review and modify local funding commitments and project timelines for grade separation projects.
  • Ensure compliance with the new state-directed funding allocation procedures.

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Public Finance (primary) - The bill’s focus on reallocating and identifying state transportation funds makes expertise in public financing and compliance critical.
  • Municipal (primary) - Local government entities such as cities and counties may need to reassess funding commitments and project management processes, requiring specialized municipal counsel.
  • Facilities and Business (secondary) - Changes in state funding and project scheduling for infrastructure projects can impact the operational aspects of facility development and business operations within affected local agencies.

Although the amendment is primarily state-directed toward high-speed rail planning and capital projects, it may indirectly affect local agencies that participate in or coordinate with these initiatives. Special District: Special districts, especially those related to transit or transportation services, may need to adjust planning and operational practices in response to shifting administrative funding procedures and potential changes in how state funds are allocated locally. While these changes target state-level oversight, they indirectly impact local public agencies that may contract with, or collaborate on, high-speed rail projects. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) County: Counties partnering with the High-Speed Rail Authority for project funding will face enhanced oversight. They must ensure that contract provisions allow IG access to key records and implement internal monitoring protocols for compliance. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Special District: Special Districts that receive high-speed rail project funds are required to update their contract templates to incorporate the specified provisions that allow IG access and facilitate periodic reviews and audits. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities formed by multiple local agencies for rail projects must integrate the mandated contract provisions across all joint agreements. The legislative change revises the funding allocation for the Reconnecting Communities: Highways to Boulevards Pilot Program. Previously, the program mandated that 25% of available funds be designated for planning and 75% for implementation. Under the new bill, agencies applying for these funds must now allocate up to 25% for planning with the remainder for implementation. This change, while adjusting funding proportions, impacts local agencies involved in transportation and infrastructure projects managed through competitive grant processes by necessitating revisions in budget planning and compliance protocols.

County: Counties engaged in transportation or infrastructure projects will need to update their funding strategies and internal controls to meet the new allocation criteria, ensuring continued eligibility for competitive grants. Special District: Special districts, particularly those related to transportation or infrastructure services, must adjust their project funding structures to align with the revised balance between planning and implementation funds. Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities, explicitly mentioned in the revised definitions, should realign their collaborative projects and funding proposals to incorporate the revised funding split, ensuring that planning costs do not exceed the permitted limit.

Key Action Items

  • Audit existing and upcoming project proposals to determine necessary budget adjustments according to the 'up to 25%' planning cap.
  • Coordinate with state DOT representatives to obtain and interpret detailed implementation guidelines of the new funding allocation.
  • Train internal staff and update procedural manuals to ensure ongoing compliance with the revised grant requirements.

Additional Requirements
