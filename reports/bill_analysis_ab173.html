Summary:
This bill revises the requirements for law enforcement agencies to request an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth, mandating that all local resources be used and that public dissemination of information could aid recovery. It affects law enforcement agencies by making the activation of an Ebony Alert by the Department of the California Highway Patrol mandatory if criteria are met. The bill also requires a report to be submitted by July 1, 2027.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • law enforcement agencies

Impact Description:
law enforcement agencies: Agencies must ensure all local resources are used and determine if public dissemination of information could assist in recovery before requesting an Ebony Alert. Department of the California Highway Patrol: The department is required to activate an Ebony Alert if the requesting agency's determinations are met and to submit a report by July 1, 2027. (Deadline: July 01, 2027)

Key Action Items
  • Train law enforcement agencies on new requirements for requesting Ebony Alerts.
  • Develop procedures for ensuring all local resources are utilized before requesting an alert.
Deadlines
  • 2027-07-01: Deadline for the Department of the California Highway Patrol to submit a report to the Governor and Legislature. (Affected agencies: Department of the California Highway Patrol)
Additional Requirements
  • Use all available local resources
  • Determine public dissemination could assist recovery
  • Activate Ebony Alert upon valid request
  • Submit report to Governor and Legislature
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Investigations (primary) - Involves law enforcement procedures and compliance with new alert activation requirements.
  • Municipal (secondary) - Involves local government compliance and inter-agency coordination for public safety alerts.

This change affects local public agencies involved in the planning and implementation of high-speed rail projects, such as cities, counties, and special districts, by allowing them to allocate more funds towards administrative costs. The impact on these agencies includes increased flexibility in managing administrative expenses related to high-speed rail projects.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • cities
  • special districts

cities: Cities involved in high-speed rail projects can allocate more funds to cover administrative costs. counties: Counties participating in high-speed rail projects can increase their administrative budget to 5% of bond proceeds. special districts: Special districts involved in rail projects can now allocate up to 5% of bond proceeds for administrative purposes.

Key Action Items

  • Ensure compliance with the new 5% limit on administrative expenses.

Summary:
This bill expands the duties of the High-Speed Rail Authority Inspector General to include reviewing or investigating adherence to contract provisions by any entity receiving high-speed rail project funds. It extends whistleblower protections to contractors' employees and mandates contract provisions for inspector general access to records and property. The bill affects the High-Speed Rail Authority and its contractors by increasing oversight and compliance requirements.

No local agencies identified.

Impact Description:
High-Speed Rail Authority: The Authority must include provisions in contracts for inspector general access to records and property, and ensure adherence to contract provisions is reviewed. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Contractors of High-Speed Rail Authority: Contractors must comply with new oversight measures and are subject to whistleblower protections.

Key Action Items
  • Review and update contract templates to include inspector general access provisions.
  • Train staff and contractors on new compliance and whistleblower protection requirements.
Deadlines
