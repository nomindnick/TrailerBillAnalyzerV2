Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. SEC. 1

The bill revises the requirements for law enforcement agencies to request an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth, mandating the use of all local resources and the availability of public-assistive information. It also requires the Department of the California Highway Patrol to activate the alert and assist the agency if the criteria are met, and mandates a report by July 1, 2027.

Agencies Impacted

law enforcement agencies: Law enforcement agencies must meet expanded criteria to request an Ebony Alert, including using all local resources and having public-assistive information. state: The Department of the California Highway Patrol is required to activate Ebony Alerts and assist law enforcement agencies if criteria are met. (Deadline: 2027-07-01)

Key Action Items
  • Law enforcement agencies must review and update procedures to meet new criteria for Ebony Alerts.
  • The Department of the California Highway Patrol must prepare to activate alerts and assist agencies as required.
Deadlines
  • 2027-07-01: Deadline for the Department of the California Highway Patrol to submit a report to the Governorâ€™s office and the Legislature. (Affected agencies: state)
Additional Requirements
  • Law enforcement agencies must use all available local resources before requesting an Ebony Alert.
  • The Department of the California Highway Patrol must activate and assist with Ebony Alerts if criteria are met.
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Technology and Privacy (primary) - The activation and dissemination of Ebony Alerts involve data privacy and technology compliance.

The bill modifies the funding allocation for the Reconnecting Communities: Highways to Boulevards Pilot Program, allowing up to 25% of available funding for planning and the remainder for implementation.

Agencies Impacted

state: The Department of Transportation must adjust the funding allocation for the pilot program. cities: Cities may need to adjust their grant applications and project planning to align with the new funding allocation. counties: Counties may need to adjust their grant applications and project planning to align with the new funding allocation.

Key Action Items
  • The Department of Transportation should update the funding guidelines for the pilot program.
  • Cities and counties should review and adjust their project proposals to align with the new funding allocation.
Additional Requirements
  • Adjust funding allocation to allow up to 25% for planning.
  • Align project proposals with new funding allocation.
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Business and Facilities (primary) - The changes affect public agency business operations and project planning related to transportation infrastructure.

Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. SEC. 13

The bill authorizes lead applicant agencies under the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to apply for a letter of no prejudice, allowing them to use their own funds on approved projects with the possibility of future reimbursement, effective until June 30, 2033.

Agencies Impacted

state: State agencies involved in the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program must comply with new provisions allowing for letters of no prejudice. (Deadline: 2033-06-30) special districts: Special districts administering or operating eligible projects can apply for letters of no prejudice to fund projects upfront. (Deadline: 2033-06-30)

Key Action Items
  • Identify eligible projects for the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program.
  • Apply for a letter of no prejudice if planning to use own funds for project components.
Deadlines
  • 2033-06-30: Provisions for letters of no prejudice become inoperative. (Affected agencies: state, special districts)
Additional Requirements
  • Apply for letter of no prejudice
  • Ensure compliance with reimbursement conditions
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Business and Facilities (primary) - The changes involve public agency business operations and funding mechanisms for infrastructure projects.

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. SEC. 2

The bill mandates the Secretary of Transportation to coordinate with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission to identify and allocate available funding for grade separation projects that lost funding due to the Budget Act of 2024. The Transportation Agency is to prioritize these projects to prevent loss of federal and local funding or project delays. A report on identified funding is due to the Legislature by April 30, 2025.

Agencies Impacted

state: The Transportation Agency, Department of Transportation, and California Transportation Commission must identify and allocate funding for specific grade separation projects. (Deadline: 2025-04-30)

Key Action Items
  • Coordinate with relevant departments to identify available funding.
  • Prioritize and allocate funding for grade separation projects at risk.
  • Prepare and submit a report to the Legislature by April 30, 2025.
Deadlines
  • 2025-04-30: Deadline for the Secretary of Transportation to report to the Legislature on identified funding for grade separation projects. (Affected agencies: state)
Additional Requirements
  • Coordinate to identify available funding
  • Prioritize and allocate funding for at-risk projects
  • Report to Legislature on identified funding
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Business and Facilities (primary) - The bill involves the allocation of funds and prioritization of transportation projects, which falls under public agency business operations and construction.

The bill mandates the Department of Transportation to report annually on zero-emission vehicles and infrastructure, starting in 2025, with a repeal date of January 1, 2036.

Agencies Impacted

state: The Department of Transportation must compile and report data on zero-emission vehicles and infrastructure. (Deadline: 2025-10-01) state: The Department of General Services must ensure a percentage of state fleet purchases are zero-emission vehicles. (Deadline: 2024-07-01)

Key Action Items
  • Department of Transportation to prepare annual reports starting 2025
  • Department of General Services to adjust procurement to meet zero-emission vehicle targets
Deadlines
  • 2025-10-01: First annual report on zero-emission vehicles due from the Department of Transportation. (Affected agencies: state)
  • 2024-07-01: Deadline for the Department of General Services to ensure 50% of light-duty vehicle purchases are zero-emission. (Affected agencies: state)
  • 2025-12-31: Deadline for 15% of heavy-duty vehicle purchases to be zero-emission. (Affected agencies: state)
Additional Requirements
  • Compile and report data on zero-emission vehicles
  • Analyze duty performance of zero-emission vehicles
  • Include information on charging and refueling stations
  • 50% of light-duty vehicles purchased must be zero-emission
  • 15% of heavy-duty vehicles purchased must be zero-emission by 2025
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Business and Facilities (primary) - The changes involve procurement and operational compliance related to vehicle fleets and infrastructure.
