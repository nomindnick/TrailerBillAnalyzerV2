Summary:
This bill revises the requirements for law enforcement agencies to request an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth, mandating that all local resources be used and that public dissemination of information could aid recovery. It affects law enforcement agencies by making the activation of an Ebony Alert by the Department of the California Highway Patrol mandatory if criteria are met. The bill also requires a report to be submitted by July 1, 2027.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • law enforcement agencies

Impact Description:
law enforcement agencies: Agencies must ensure all local resources are used and determine if public dissemination of information could assist in recovery before requesting an Ebony Alert. Department of the California Highway Patrol: The department is required to activate an Ebony Alert if the requesting agency's criteria are met and to submit a report by July 1, 2027. (Deadline: July 01, 2027)

Key Action Items
  • Train law enforcement agencies on new criteria for requesting Ebony Alerts.
  • Develop procedures for documenting the use of local resources and public dissemination determinations.
Deadlines
  • 2027-07-01: Deadline for the Department of the California Highway Patrol to submit a report to the Governor and Legislature. (Affected agencies: Department of the California Highway Patrol)
Additional Requirements
  • Use all available local resources
  • Determine public dissemination could assist recovery
  • Activate Ebony Alert if criteria met
  • Submit report to Governor and Legislature
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Investigations (primary) - Involves law enforcement procedures and compliance with new alert activation requirements.
  • Municipal (secondary) - Involves local government operations and inter-agency coordination for public safety.

Summary:
The bill modifies the Reconnecting Communities: Highways to Boulevards Pilot Program by changing the funding allocation requirement. Previously, 25% of the funding was set aside for planning and 75% for implementation. The bill now allows up to 25% for planning, with the remainder for implementation. This change affects local public agencies such as cities, counties, and special districts that are eligible for these grants, as it provides more flexibility in how they can allocate funds between planning and implementation phases. These agencies are impacted by having the potential to allocate more resources towards implementation, which could accelerate project timelines and enhance project outcomes.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • cities
  • special districts
  • counties

Impact Description:
cities: Cities can now allocate more than 75% of grant funds towards implementation, potentially speeding up project completion. counties: Counties have increased flexibility in funding allocation, allowing for more resources to be directed towards project implementation. special districts: Special districts can allocate more funds towards implementation, enhancing project delivery capabilities.

Key Action Items
  • Review and adjust grant application strategies to maximize funding for implementation.
  • Coordinate with the Department of Transportation to understand new funding allocation guidelines.
Additional Requirements
  • Apply for grants
  • Plan and implement projects
