Board Governance

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: No specific sections found.

The legislative change mandates that law enforcement agencies must exhaust all local resources and have actionable public information before requesting an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth. The Department of the California Highway Patrol is required to activate the alert if these conditions are met. A report on the implementation is due by July 1, 2027.

Local Agencies Impacted

No specific local agencies identified.

Local Agency Impact

Law Enforcement Agencies: Agencies must ensure all local resources are used and have actionable information before requesting an Ebony Alert. Department of the California Highway Patrol: The department is required to activate the Ebony Alert if the requesting agency meets the new criteria.

Key Action Items
  • Review and update protocols for requesting Ebony Alerts to ensure compliance with new requirements.
  • Train law enforcement personnel on the revised criteria and procedures for activating Ebony Alerts.

Public Finance

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: No specific sections found.

The bill mandates the Secretary of Transportation to coordinate with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission to identify and allocate available state transportation funds for grade separation projects that lost funding due to the Budget Act of 2024. The Transportation Agency is tasked with prioritizing and potentially directly allocating these funds to projects at risk of losing federal and local funding or facing schedule delays. A report on identified funding must be submitted to the Legislature by April 30, 2025.

Local Agencies Impacted

No specific local agencies identified.

Local Agency Impact

Transportation Agency: The agency must identify and prioritize funding for grade separation projects that lost funding and are at risk of losing additional funding or facing delays. (Deadline: April 30, 2025)

Key Action Items
  • Coordinate with relevant departments to identify available funding for grade separation projects.
  • Prepare a report detailing identified funding and submit it to the Legislature by the deadline.

Business and Facilities

The legislative change mandates the Department of Transportation to report annually on zero-emission vehicles and their performance, starting in 2025, with provisions expiring in 2036.

Local Agencies Impacted

No specific local agencies identified.

Local Agency Impact

Department of Transportation: The Department of Transportation must compile and report data on zero-emission vehicles and analyze their performance annually. (Deadline: October 01, 2025)

Key Action Items
  • Establish a system for tracking zero-emission vehicle acquisitions and performance.
  • Develop a reporting framework to meet the annual October 1 deadline.
  • Plan for the installation and maintenance of zero-emission vehicle charging and refueling stations.

Trailer Bill Sections: No specific sections found.

The bill authorizes the Department of General Services, with the Department of Motor Vehicles' consent, to enter into a lease-purchase or lease with an option to purchase agreement for a new build-to-suit office facility in San Francisco, potentially including affordable housing. The lease requires approval by the Department of Finance and adherence to legislative notice requirements and the Property Acquisition Law.

Local Agencies Impacted

No specific local agencies identified.

Local Agency Impact

State Agency: The Department of General Services can now engage in lease-purchase agreements for a new DMV office in San Francisco, potentially affecting operational planning and real estate management.

Key Action Items
  • Coordinate with the Department of Motor Vehicles to obtain necessary consent.
  • Prepare documentation for Department of Finance approval.
  • Ensure compliance with legislative notice requirements and the Property Acquisition Law.
Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: No specific sections found.

The legislative change expands the duties of the High-Speed Rail Authority Inspector General to include reviewing or investigating adherence to contract provisions by entities receiving high-speed rail project funds. It extends whistleblower protections to contractor employees and mandates inclusion of access provisions in contracts from July 1, 2024.

Local Agencies Impacted

No specific local agencies identified.

Local Agency Impact

High-Speed Rail Authority: The Authority must ensure all contracts and amendments include provisions granting the Inspector General access to records and property. (Deadline: July 01, 2024) Contractors of High-Speed Rail Authority: Contractors must comply with new oversight and whistleblower protection requirements.

Key Action Items
  • Review and update contract templates to include required access provisions by July 1, 2024.
  • Train relevant staff on new oversight and whistleblower protection requirements.

The legislative change allows lead applicant agencies under the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to apply for a letter of no prejudice, enabling them to use their own funds on approved projects with the possibility of future reimbursement from program funds, effective until June 30, 2033.

Local Agencies Impacted

No specific local agencies identified.

Local Agency Impact

Key Action Items

  • Prepare applications for a letter of no prejudice to the Secretary of Transportation.
