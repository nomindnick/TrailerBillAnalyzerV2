Local Agency Impact

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. SEC. 1

The bill revises the requirements for law enforcement agencies to request an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth, mandating the use of all local resources and the availability of public-assistive information. It also requires the Department of the California Highway Patrol to activate the alert and assist the agency if the criteria are met, and mandates a report to the Governor and Legislature by July 1, 2027.

Agencies Impacted

law enforcement agencies: Law enforcement agencies must meet expanded criteria to request an Ebony Alert, including using all local resources and having public-assistive information. state agencies: The Department of the California Highway Patrol is required to activate Ebony Alerts and assist law enforcement agencies if criteria are met.

Key Action Items
  • Review and update criteria for requesting Ebony Alerts.
  • Ensure all local resources are utilized before requesting an alert.
  • Prepare for mandatory activation and assistance by the Department of the California Highway Patrol.
Deadlines
  • 2027-07-01: Deadline for the Department of the California Highway Patrol to submit a report to the Governor and Legislature. (Affected agencies: state agencies)
Additional Requirements
  • Use all available local resources
  • Have information that could assist in recovery
  • Mandatory activation and assistance by the Department of the California Highway Patrol
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Technology and Privacy (secondary) - Involves dissemination of alert messages and signs, which may involve technology and data privacy considerations.
  • Board Governance (secondary) - Involves compliance with new legislative requirements and reporting to the Governor and Legislature.

Additional Requirements

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Public Finance (secondary) - The practice group deals with public agency financing, which is relevant to the allocation and use of grant funds.
Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. SEC. 13

The bill allows lead applicant agencies under the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to apply for a letter of no prejudice, enabling them to use their own funds on approved projects with the possibility of future reimbursement, effective until June 30, 2033.

Agencies Impacted

state agencies: State agencies involved in the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program must comply with new provisions allowing for letters of no prejudice. (Deadline: 2033-06-30) local public agencies: Local public agencies administering eligible projects can apply for a letter of no prejudice to use their own funds with potential reimbursement. (Deadline: 2033-06-30)

Key Action Items
  • State and local agencies should assess eligibility for applying for a letter of no prejudice.
  • Develop procedures to track expenditures for potential reimbursement.
Deadlines
  • 2033-06-30: Provisions for letters of no prejudice become inoperative. (Affected agencies: state agencies, local public agencies)
  • 2034-01-01: Provisions for letters of no prejudice are repealed. (Affected agencies: state agencies, local public agencies)
Additional Requirements
  • Apply for a letter of no prejudice
  • Ensure compliance with reimbursement conditions
  • Partner with private operators if necessary
  • Meet conditions for reimbursement
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Business and Facilities (primary) - The changes involve public agency business operations and funding mechanisms for infrastructure projects.
  • Public Finance (secondary) - The bill involves financial management and reimbursement processes for public agencies.

State Agency Impact

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. SEC. 2

The bill mandates the Secretary of Transportation to coordinate with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission to identify and allocate available funding for grade separation projects that lost funding due to the Budget Act of 2024. The Transportation Agency is required to prioritize these projects, especially those at risk of losing federal and local funding or facing schedule delays. A report on the identified funding must be submitted to the Legislature by April 30, 2025.

Agencies Impacted

state agencies: The Transportation Agency must identify and allocate funding for specific grade separation projects. (Deadline: 2025-04-30)

Key Action Items
  • Coordinate with relevant departments to identify funding
  • Prioritize and allocate funding for grade separation projects
  • Prepare and submit a report to the Legislature by April 30, 2025
Deadlines
  • 2025-04-30: Deadline for the Secretary of Transportation to report to the Legislature on identified funding for grade separation projects. (Affected agencies: state agencies)
Additional Requirements
  • Coordinate with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission
  • Identify available funding for grade separation projects
  • Prioritize and allocate funding for at-risk projects
  • Report to the Legislature on identified funding
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Business and Facilities (primary) - The practice group handles public agency business operations and construction, which are relevant to the allocation and prioritization of transportation project funding.
  • Public Finance (secondary) - The practice group deals with public agency financing, which is relevant to the identification and allocation of funding for transportation projects.

The bill mandates the Department of Transportation to report annually on zero-emission vehicles and infrastructure, starting in 2025, with provisions expiring in 2036.

Agencies Impacted

state agencies: The Department of Transportation must compile and report data on zero-emission vehicles and infrastructure. (Deadline: 2025-10-01) state agencies: State agencies must transition a portion of their vehicle fleets to zero-emission vehicles. (Deadline: 2025-12-31)

Key Action Items
  • Develop a system for annual reporting on zero-emission vehicles by 2025.
  • Ensure compliance with zero-emission vehicle purchase requirements by 2025.
Deadlines
  • 2025-10-01: First annual report on zero-emission vehicles due to the Legislature. (Affected agencies: state agencies)
  • 2025-12-31: Deadline for 15% of new medium- and heavy-duty vehicles to be zero-emission. (Affected agencies: state agencies)
Additional Requirements
  • Annual reporting on zero-emission vehicles
  • Analysis of vehicle duty performance
  • Information on charging and refueling stations
  • 15% of new medium- and heavy-duty vehicles must be zero-emission
  • 50% of new light-duty vehicles must be zero-emission
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Business and Facilities (primary) - The practice group handles public agency business operations and procurement, relevant to vehicle fleet management and infrastructure.
