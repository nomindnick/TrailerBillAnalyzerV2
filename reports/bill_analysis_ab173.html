Summary:
This bill revises the requirements for law enforcement agencies to request an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth, mandating that all local resources be used and that public dissemination of information could aid recovery. It affects law enforcement agencies by making the activation of an Ebony Alert by the Department of the California Highway Patrol mandatory if criteria are met. The bill also requires a report to be submitted by July 1, 2027.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • law enforcement agencies

Impact Description:
law enforcement agencies: Agencies must ensure all local resources are used and determine if public dissemination could assist in recovery before requesting an Ebony Alert. Department of the California Highway Patrol: The department is required to activate an Ebony Alert if the requesting agency's criteria are met and to submit a report by July 1, 2027. (Deadline: July 01, 2027)

Key Action Items
  • Train law enforcement agencies on new criteria for requesting Ebony Alerts.
  • Develop procedures for documenting the use of local resources and public dissemination determinations.
Deadlines
  • 2027-07-01: Deadline for the Department of the California Highway Patrol to submit a report to the Governor and Legislature. (Affected agencies: Department of the California Highway Patrol)
Additional Requirements
  • Use all available local resources
  • Determine public dissemination could assist recovery
  • Activate Ebony Alert if criteria met
  • Submit report to Governor and Legislature
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Investigations (primary) - Involves law enforcement procedures and compliance with new alert activation requirements.
  • Municipal (secondary) - Involves local government agencies and their interaction with state departments for public safety.

Summary:
This bill modifies the funding allocation requirements for the Reconnecting Communities: Highways to Boulevards Pilot Program, changing the mandate from a fixed 25% of funds for planning to allowing up to 25% for planning, with the remainder for implementation. This affects local public agencies such as cities, counties, and special districts that may apply for these grants to transform underutilized highways into multimodal corridors. These agencies are impacted by having more flexibility in how they allocate grant funds between planning and implementation, potentially accelerating project timelines and focusing more resources on implementation.

  • counties
  • cities
  • special districts

Impact Description:
cities: Cities can now allocate more funds towards implementation rather than being restricted to a fixed 25% for planning. counties: Counties can now allocate more funds towards implementation rather than being restricted to a fixed 25% for planning. special districts: Special districts can now allocate more funds towards implementation rather than being restricted to a fixed 25% for planning.
