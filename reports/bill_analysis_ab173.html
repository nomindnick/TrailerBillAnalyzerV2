The legislative change mandates that law enforcement agencies must exhaust all local resources and have actionable public information before requesting an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth. The Department of the California Highway Patrol is required to activate the alert if these conditions are met. A report on the implementation is due by July 1, 2027.

Agencies Impacted

Key Action Items
  • Train law enforcement personnel on new requirements for requesting Ebony Alerts.
  • Develop protocols to ensure all local resources are utilized before requesting an alert.
Deadlines
  • 2027-07-01: Submission of report on Ebony Alert implementation to the Governorâ€™s office and the Legislature. (Affected agencies: Department of the California Highway Patrol)
Additional Requirements
  • Law enforcement agencies must use all available local resources before requesting an Ebony Alert.
  • The Department of the California Highway Patrol must activate the alert if the agency's request meets the new criteria.
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Board Governance (primary)
  • Technology and Privacy (secondary)

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC.

The legislative change mandates the Secretary of Transportation to coordinate with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission to identify and allocate available state transportation funding for grade separation projects that lost funding due to the Budget Act of 2024. The Transportation Agency is tasked with prioritizing and potentially directly allocating funds to projects at risk of losing federal and local funding or facing schedule delays. A report on identified funding must be submitted to the Legislature by April 30, 2025.

Agencies Impacted

Key Action Items
  • Coordinate with relevant agencies to identify available funding for grade separation projects.
  • Prioritize projects at risk of losing funding or facing delays.
  • Prepare and submit a report to the Legislature by April 30, 2025.
Deadlines
  • 2025-04-30: Report on identified funding for impacted grade separation projects due to the Legislature. (Affected agencies: Transportation Agency)
Additional Requirements
  • Coordinate with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission.
  • Identify and prioritize available funding for grade separation projects.
  • Submit a report to the Legislature by April 30, 2025.

The legislative change allows lead applicant agencies under the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to apply for a letter of no prejudice from the Secretary of Transportation. This permits them to use their own funds on approved projects with the possibility of future reimbursement from program funds, provided certain conditions are met. This provision is inoperative after June 30, 2033, and repealed on January 1, 2034.

Agencies Impacted

Key Action Items
  • Identify eligible projects that could benefit from early funding.
  • Prepare applications for a letter of no prejudice to the Secretary of Transportation.
  • Review and ensure compliance with conditions for reimbursement.
Deadlines
  • 2033-06-30: Provisions for the letter of no prejudice become inoperative. (Affected agencies: Public agencies administering or operating eligible projects)
Additional Requirements
  • Apply for a letter of no prejudice from the Secretary of Transportation.
  • Meet conditions for reimbursement to ensure eligibility for future program funds.
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Business and Facilities (primary)
  • Public Finance (primary)
