Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. SEC. 1

The bill revises the requirements for law enforcement agencies to request an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth, mandating that all local resources be used and that public dissemination of information could aid in recovery. It also requires the Department of the California Highway Patrol to activate the alert if these conditions are met and to report to the Governor and Legislature by July 1, 2027.

Agencies Impacted

state: The Department of the California Highway Patrol must activate Ebony Alerts if conditions are met and report to the Governor and Legislature by July 1, 2027. (Deadline: 2027-07-01) law enforcement agencies: Law enforcement agencies must use all local resources and determine public dissemination could aid recovery before requesting an Ebony Alert.

Key Action Items
  • Law enforcement agencies should review and update procedures for requesting Ebony Alerts.
  • The Department of the California Highway Patrol should prepare to meet the reporting requirement by July 1, 2027.
Deadlines
  • 2027-07-01: Deadline for the Department of the California Highway Patrol to submit a report to the Governor and Legislature. (Affected agencies: state)
Additional Requirements
  • Law enforcement agencies must use all local resources before requesting an Ebony Alert.
  • The Department of the California Highway Patrol must activate alerts and report to the Governor and Legislature.
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Technology and Privacy (primary) - Involves dissemination of information and data privacy considerations.

Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. SEC. 2

The bill requires the Secretary of Transportation to coordinate with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission to identify and allocate available funding for grade separation projects that lost funding due to the Budget Act of 2024. The Transportation Agency is tasked with prioritizing and potentially directly allocating funds to projects at risk of losing federal and local funding or facing schedule delays. A report on identified funding must be submitted to the Legislature by April 30, 2025.

Agencies Impacted

state: The Transportation Agency must identify and allocate funding for grade separation projects. (Deadline: 2025-04-30)

Key Action Items
  • Coordinate with relevant departments to identify available funding.
  • Prioritize grade separation projects at risk of losing funding.
  • Prepare and submit a report to the Legislature by April 30, 2025.
Deadlines
  • 2025-04-30: Deadline for the Secretary of Transportation to report to the Legislature on identified funding for grade separation projects. (Affected agencies: state)
Additional Requirements
  • Coordinate with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission.
  • Identify and allocate funding for grade separation projects.
  • Submit a report to the Legislature by April 30, 2025.
Relevant Practice Groups

The bill authorizes the Department of General Services, with the consent of the Department of Motor Vehicles, to enter into a lease-purchase agreement or lease with an option to purchase for a new DMV office facility in San Francisco. The project may include mixed-use development with affordable housing and requires approval by the Department of Finance and compliance with legislative notice requirements and the Property Acquisition Law.

Agencies Impacted

  • Ensure compliance with legislative notice requirements and the Property Acquisition Law.
Additional Requirements

  • Ensure lease is approved by the Department of Finance

  • Adhere to the Property Acquisition Law

Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. SEC. 13

The bill allows lead applicant agencies under the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program to apply for a letter of no prejudice from the Secretary of Transportation. This permits them to use their own funds on approved projects with the possibility of future reimbursement from program funds, provided certain conditions are met. These provisions will become inoperative on June 30, 2033, and will be repealed on January 1, 2034.

Agencies Impacted

state: State agencies administering the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program must process applications for letters of no prejudice and manage reimbursements. public agencies: Public agencies can apply for a letter of no prejudice to use their own funds on projects with potential reimbursement.

Key Action Items
  • Public agencies should evaluate projects for potential early funding using their own resources.
  • State agencies need to establish procedures for processing letters of no prejudice and managing reimbursements.
Deadlines
  • 2033-06-30: Provisions for letters of no prejudice become inoperative. (Affected agencies: state, public agencies)
  • 2034-01-01: Provisions for letters of no prejudice are repealed. (Affected agencies: state, public agencies)
Additional Requirements
  • Apply for a letter of no prejudice
  • Ensure compliance with conditions for reimbursement
  • Process applications for letters of no prejudice
  • Manage reimbursement processes
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Business and Facilities (primary) - The changes involve public agency business operations and funding mechanisms for infrastructure projects.
