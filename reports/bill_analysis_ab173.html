Public Finance

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: No specific sections found.

The bill authorizes lead applicant agencies to apply for a letter of no prejudice, allowing them to use their own funds on approved projects with the possibility of future reimbursement from program funds.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • cities
  • counties
  • special districts

Local Agency Impact

Local public agencies that are lead applicants in the Port and Freight Infrastructure Program can now advance projects using their own funds with the potential for reimbursement, which may accelerate project timelines and require careful financial planning.

Analysis

<ul> <li>The proposed change allows local agencies to expedite port and freight infrastructure projects by using their own funds upfront. This can be beneficial in advancing project timelines but requires agencies to ensure they meet the conditions for reimbursement. Agencies must assess their financial capacity to front costs and manage the risk of non-reimbursement if conditions are not met. Legal counsel should review the terms of the letter of no prejudice and ensure compliance with all program requirements.</li> </ul>
Key Action Items
  • Evaluate financial capacity to fund projects upfront and manage cash flow implications.
  • Apply for a letter of no prejudice from the Secretary of Transportation if planning to use own funds.
  • Ensure compliance with all conditions for reimbursement to mitigate financial risk.
  • Review and update project timelines and budgets to reflect potential accelerated schedules.
  • Consult with legal counsel to understand the terms and conditions of the letter of no prejudice.

General Local Agency Impact

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: No specific sections found.

The bill revises the criteria for law enforcement agencies to request an Ebony Alert, requiring them to exhaust local resources and have actionable public information, and mandates the Department of the California Highway Patrol to activate the alert if criteria are met.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • law enforcement agencies

Local Agency Impact

Local law enforcement agencies will need to ensure they have utilized all local resources and have actionable information before requesting an Ebony Alert. This may require additional documentation and coordination efforts.

Analysis

<ul> <li>The proposed change imposes new requirements on local law enforcement agencies to demonstrate that all local resources have been exhausted and that there is actionable information before requesting an Ebony Alert. This could lead to increased administrative work to document these efforts. Additionally, the Department of the California Highway Patrol is now required to activate the alert if the criteria are met, which may streamline the process but also necessitates thorough preparation by local agencies to meet the new standards. Agencies should prepare for potential audits or reviews of their compliance with these requirements.</li> </ul>
Key Action Items
  • Review and update protocols for requesting Ebony Alerts to ensure compliance with new requirements.
  • Train staff on the new criteria and documentation needed to demonstrate exhaustion of local resources and availability of actionable information.
  • Establish a checklist or system to track the use of local resources in missing person cases involving Black youth.
  • Coordinate with the Department of the California Highway Patrol to understand the new mandatory activation process.
  • Prepare for potential reporting or audits by maintaining detailed records of all steps taken in the alert process.

Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: Section 15

The bill expands the accountability program requirements to include funds from the Greenhouse Gas Reduction Fund and mandates updated financial plans and data submissions for additional fiscal years.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • regional transportation planning agencies

Local Agency Impact

Regional transportation planning agencies must comply with expanded reporting requirements to access funds, including submitting updated financial plans and transit data for additional fiscal years.

Analysis

<ul> <li>The proposed change requires regional transportation planning agencies to submit updated regional short-term financial plans and transit operator data to continue receiving funds under the expanded accountability program. This includes funds from the Greenhouse Gas Reduction Fund, which adds a new layer of compliance for these agencies. The Transportation Agency is also authorized to modify guidelines for fund distribution, which may require agencies to adapt to new compliance standards. Agencies must ensure timely and accurate submissions to secure funding for the 2025–26, 2026–27, and 2027–28 fiscal years.</li> </ul>
Key Action Items
  • Review and update regional short-term financial plans to meet new requirements.
  • Ensure accurate and timely submission of transit operator data for the specified fiscal years.
  • Monitor any guideline changes issued by the Transportation Agency and adjust compliance strategies accordingly.
  • Coordinate with the Transportation Agency to understand new requirements related to the Greenhouse Gas Reduction Fund.
  • Prepare internal teams for ongoing compliance and reporting obligations through 2028.

  • special districts

Local Agency Impact

This could accelerate project timelines and enhance the scope of transformations from highways to multimodal corridors. Agencies should assess their current and planned projects to determine if they can benefit from this increased flexibility in funding allocation. Additionally, they should prepare to adjust their grant applications and project plans to align with the new funding structure.</li> </ul>
Key Action Items

  • Adjust grant applications to reflect the potential for more implementation funding.
  • Coordinate with the Department of Transportation to understand new application guidelines.
  • Evaluate project timelines and scopes to maximize the benefits of the new funding flexibility.
  • Communicate with stakeholders about potential changes in project funding and timelines.
