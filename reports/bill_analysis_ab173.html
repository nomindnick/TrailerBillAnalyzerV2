Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SECTION 1.

Summary:
This bill modifies Government Code Section 8594.14 to expand and revise the criteria law enforcement agencies must use when requesting an Ebony Alert for missing Black youth aged 12-25. City police departments and county sheriff departments are primarily affected, as they must now determine that all local resources have been exhausted and that public dissemination of information could assist in recovery. The bill also changes CHP's response from discretionary to mandatory when they concur with the law enforcement agency's determination that an alert is warranted, requiring them to activate the alert and assist with dissemination.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • Law enforcement agencies (city police departments, county sheriff departments)

Impact Description:
Law enforcement agencies (city police departments, county sheriff departments): Must follow more specific criteria when determining whether to request an Ebony Alert, including verifying local resources have been exhausted and that public information dissemination would be helpful

Key Action Items
  • Review and update policies related to missing persons cases and Ebony Alert requests
  • Train law enforcement personnel on new criteria for requesting Ebony Alerts
  • Develop protocols to ensure proper documentation of local resource exhaustion before requesting alerts
Additional Requirements
  • Law enforcement agencies must determine information is available that could assist in recovery if disseminated
  • Agencies must verify all available local resources have been used before requesting an Ebony Alert
  • Criteria for determining suspicious circumstances or danger must be broadly construed as specified in the law
  • Missing person must be between ages 12-25
Relevant Practice Groups
  • Municipal (primary) - Directly affects city and county law enforcement operations and procedures
  • Governance (secondary) - Relates to procedural requirements for public agencies

Regional transportation planning agencies (RTPAs) are primarily affected, as they must now submit updated financial plans and transit operator data for the 2025-26 fiscal year, and updated transit operator data for the 2026-27 and 2027-28 fiscal years to receive program funding. Summary:
This bill modifies the Reconnecting Communities: Highways to Boulevards Pilot Program by changing the funding allocation from a fixed 25% for planning and 75% for implementation to 'up to 25%' for planning and the remainder for implementation. This change impacts local agencies, regional agencies, joint powers authorities, tribal governments, and transit agencies that are eligible to apply for grants under this program. The modification provides the Department of Transportation with greater flexibility to allocate more funding toward implementation projects rather than planning, potentially benefiting agencies that are ready to implement projects while possibly reducing available planning funds.
