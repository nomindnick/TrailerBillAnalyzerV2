Substantive Change
Trailer Bill Sections: SEC. 2.

Summary:
The legislative change requires the Secretary of Transportation to work with the Department of Transportation and the California Transportation Commission to identify available state funds that can support grade separation projects at risk of losing local and federal funding. Although the bill targets state-level funding mechanisms, its effects may filter down to local agencies by altering funding commitments and necessitating improved coordination between local and state entities.

Local Agencies Impacted

  • City
  • County
  • Special District
  • Joint Powers Authority

Impact Description:
City: Cities with grade separation projects may need to reassess local funding commitments as the state reallocates funds to cover projects at risk of delayed schedules or losing local funding. This could require enhanced coordination with state agencies and adjustments in local budgeting procedures. County: Counties with transportation projects may be indirectly impacted by the state’s reallocation of funds. This reallocation could require counties to audit their current funding strategies and actively engage with state agencies to realign project priorities. Special District: Special districts engaged in local transportation services may need to modify their funding mechanisms if state funds are redirected to support grade separation projects. They should ensure compliance with new state allocation procedures and reassess grant and funding strategies. Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities that oversee transportation initiatives across multiple local jurisdictions may face changes in collaborative funding arrangements. They will need to re-evaluate interagency agreements in light of potential shifts towards state-managed funding for grade separation projects.

Key Action Items

  • Establish early coordination channels with state transportation agencies
  • Review and adjust local funding commitments for grade separation projects
  • Monitor the state report due by April 30, 2025 for guidance on funding allocations

Deadlines

  • 2025-04-30: Deadline for the Secretary of Transportation to report to the Legislature on funding identified for at-risk grade separation projects. Local agencies should monitor this report to adjust their funding strategies accordingly. (Affected agencies: City, County, Special District, Joint Powers Authority)

Additional Requirements

  • Establish coordination mechanisms with state transportation agencies
  • Conduct audits of current transportation funding commitments
  • Adjust local budgets in response to state fund reallocation
  • Monitor legislative reporting deadlines for further guidance

Relevant Practice Groups

  • Municipal (primary) - Cities, counties, and special districts are directly involved in local infrastructure projects and will need to adjust their funding and coordination strategies as state funds are reallocated.
  • Public Finance (secondary) - The reallocation of state funds and potential shifts in local budgeting require expertise in public finance to manage and realign funding commitments effectively.

This expansion requires regional transportation planning agencies—a category which often includes local entities organized as Cities, Counties, Special Districts, or Joint Powers Authorities—to update and submit financial plans and transit operator data in order to access funds in upcoming fiscal years. Local agencies affected must review their compliance, reporting, and data management practices in anticipation of modified distribution guidelines and new submission deadlines.

Special District: Special districts that serve as or participate in regional transportation planning agencies will need to adjust their internal processes to meet the updated reporting obligations for receiving funds. Joint Powers Authority: Joint Powers Authorities, which often are created by local entities for coordinated transportation planning, must now comply with additional data submission requirements and be prepared for guideline modifications by the Transportation Agency.
